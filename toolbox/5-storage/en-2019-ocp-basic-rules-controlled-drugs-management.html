Basic rules on CONTROLLED DRUGS management

Controlled drugs include all narcotics and psychotropic drugs. The medicines included in these categories
may vary between countries and therefore it is important to have a current list available from the
Ministry of Health.

These drugs are subject to strict regulations. However, there are not standard rules and regulations are
according to every country with huge disparities;

Drugs considered controlled for all MSF Missions:

- Analgesics: Morphine, Fentanyl
- Anesthetics: Ketamine, Midazolam
- Anticonvulsants & anesthetics: Diazepam, Phenobarbital

Other drugs considered controlled for some missions based on national regulation: tramadol, thiopental,
codeine, ephedrine, etc.

Therefore MSF has to put in place a minimum for a good control. If controlled drugs come from MSF
Logistique, there are some particular export conditions according to the French law.

The following points are guidance on how to manage controlled drugs in order to fulfill general country
regulations.


AT MAIN PHARMACY LEVEL:

 Controlled drugs should be kept in a cupboard with lock.

 The key should be present only with the person in charge of the main pharmacy (pharmacy

manager).

 The order request should be done on the appropriate written document, with a specific/separated

packing list, and delivered to the unit hand to hand.

 A controlled drug register is mandatory and it should be kept inside the cupboard (depending on the

country, a specific national register could be available).

 The register should be updated immediately after receipt or deliver.

 Stock cards: even tough are not mandatory (as the register is in place already), they are

recommended.

 The register should show the name of the receiving/sending party (either the hospital unit or other

entity).

 At any given time the stock as shown in the register should tally with the physical stock.

 Expired control drugs must be kept inside the cupboard but should be packed in a separate box and

labeled ‘EXPIRED CONTROLLED DRUGS FOR DESTRUCTION’.

 Monthly complete inventory should be done systematically.

 List of items considered as controlled drugs in the country should be available.

AT WARDS LEVEL:

 Control drugs should be kept in a cupboard or appropriate metallic box with lock (the metallic box

has to be fixed on the wall).

 One person should be responsible and the key should be present only with that person (usually is the

unit supervisor).

 A control drug register is mandatory and it has to be kept inside the cupboard.

 The register should be updated immediately after receipt or issues.

 The register should show the details of the patient and prescriber.

 At any given time the stock as shown in the register should tally with the physical stock.

 In some countries the empty ampoules or vials should be collected after use.

 Non finished vials should be returned to the main pharmacy for their destruction.

 Expired control drugs should be returned to the main pharmacy with the appropriate document for

future destruction.

 Inventory should be done systematically before every request sent to the main pharmacy.

 List of prescribers should be available and visible (usually in MSF settings the prescribers are

anesthetists, doctors and surgeons).

 List of items considered as controlled drugs in the country should be available and visible for the

medical team.
