Emergency plan

List of people to call in the order specified below (if no immediate answer or no news after 15mn, call the next one) :

Position  Name Telephone
1 Logistic Team Leader
2 Log Capital
3 Pharmacy supervisor
4 Medical supervisor

  • In the event of a power failure (witness lamp off):

Here elaborate a backup plan in case of main power source failure:

e.g.: The guard checks regularly the witness lamp (indicate the number), when the light goes out, starts the backup generator (indicate the number and their identification). If there are problems starting the backup generator, the watchman must call the persons in the table above.

Here elaborate a backup plan in case the planned backup does not work:

e.g.: The Logistic team leader must organize to take a 6 kVA generator from the stock and install it in the Pharmacy to supply exclusively the cold chain. A 20 m extension cord must always be available with generator..

  • In case of failure of one or more equipment :

Here elaborate a contingency plan in case of equipment failure. Note that here you have to make a hypothesis of what could happen. It is unlikely that all equipment will fail at the same time. You can choose a ratio such as 1 out of 10 equipment for example, but it must be determined according to the context.

This situation will be detected when using the equipment or when taking temperature readings. If the cold room fails, the audible alarm will go off. The person in charge must call the relevant people according to the table above.

Evaluation of the breakdown and the time to repair (Responsible = Log):

  • If the time to repair is less than the autonomy of the equipment: LEAVE THE PRODUCTS IN THE ACTIVE COLD CHAIN EQUIPMENT while the equipment is repaired.

  • If the time to repair is longer than the autonomy of the equipment, follow the instructions below:

  1. Refrigerator :

Transfer the products (Responsible = Pharmacy Supervisor).

Reminder – if 1 or more monitors are in alarm :

  • Press the "mark" button on the LotgTag so that a mark appears on the recorded curve.

  • Immediately place the items in quarantine. Place them inside another medical refrigerator or passive cold chain in order to keep them between 2 and 8°C. Don’t forget to transfer the respective LogTag and Freeze Tag.

  • Clearly label "DO NOT USE UNTIL NEW ORDER / COLD CHAIN BREAK".

Here elaborate a contingency plan if the equipment(s) failed:

A few examples:

  • If you have storage capacity in one or more other refrigerator(s): transfer the products, the most sensitive ones first. Make sure to identify them properly to avoid confusion.

  • Transfer the products in RCW25 or insulated boxes (see inventory table above and annexes xx.) and at the same time install the emergency equipment in stock.

  • Transfer the products to a partner's cold chain (other section, NGO, EPI, etc.)

Cold room:

if the breakdown is on one refrigeration unit, you can switch to the second unit so that you don't have to empty the cold room.

If the estimated breakdown is more than 40 hours: plan for the renewal of the icepacks for the passive cold chain.

Put the products back into the repaired/backup equipment (Responsible = Pharmacy Supervisor) :

Before putting them back in, check that the inside temperature is within the 2 to 8 °C range for 24 hours.

  1. Freezer

Transfer the icepacks (Responsible = Logistics or Pharmacy Supervisor) :

Here develop a contingency plan if the freezer breaks down:

A few examples:

  • If you have storage capacity in one or more other freezers: Transfer the ice packs to this (these) freezer(s).

  • Install the backup freezer

  • Transfer the icepacks to a passive cold chain while buying a new freezer - local supplier identified in advance.

Put the icepacks back into the repaired/backup equipment (Person in charge = Logistician or Pharmacy Supervisor):

Before putting them back in, check that the inside temperature is within the -15 to -30°C range.
