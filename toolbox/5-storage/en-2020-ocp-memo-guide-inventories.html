Général

Why conduct INVENTORIE ?

Inventories are essential for :

- Check that the quantities in stock (physical stock) and the expiry dates are the same as those noted on the stock card and in the management software (theoretical stock).

- Correct if necessary the stock card and the management software (Isystock).

- Identify the causes of discrepancies (theoretical / physical stocks) and implement corrective actions to avoid them.

- Check the absence of expired or deteriorated products and remove them from stock if there are any.

- Perform the following analyses: identify pre-expired, sleeping and overstock.

- Valorize the stock.

- Valorize inexplicable discrepancies and implementing corrective actions to avoid them

- Evaluate the performance of the management team:

  • For inventory evaluation indicators, see the Inventory chapter of the monthly report.

  • Poor indicators reflect poor management on the part of the pharmacy team.

Inventories are also an opportunity to check:

- The storage of each product in its place (position label) and according to its expiry date.

- The non-dispersion of reserve stocks.

(Example: cartons "tidied / hidden" at the bottom of the warehouse found, etc.).

différent types of inventories

Complete inventory: concerns all items in stock.

Partial inventory: Only a part of the items in stock.

FREQUENCy of inventories

  • Complete inventory:

At least every 3 months, or before each order if the periodicity = 4

If the periodicity of orders = 4 months => Full inventory every 4 months

Periodicity of orders > 4 months => Full inventory every 3 months

The objective is to know exactly what stock is available for each of the articles but also to know the validity of this stock: we can have a sufficient quantity but it may expire, in whole or in part, before receipt of the next order ("Lead o + Period to Cover + Safety Stock").

Partial inventories:

To be carried out each month when a complete inventory is not scheduled, at least 10% of the number of items in stock.

Depending on the type of project, systematically or by alternation, partial inventories may concern:

- The most important items for the project:

  • If HIV/AIDS project -> Anti-Retroviral (ARVs)

  • If TB project -> Anti-tuberculosis drugs

  • If MDR-TB project -> MDR-TB drugs

For the other projects -> define the list of the most important items with the Project Medical Manager or the Medical Coordinator; which can be the products with a high turnover (products with the highest consumption, determined according to the ABC method, 10% of the items represent 70% of the financial movements), Or the vital products defined for example according to the VEN classification.

- A defined / particular category or geographical area of the pharmacy.

  • Psychotropic & Narcotic drugs: their inventory is mandatory (see Memo basic rules of controlled drugs).

  • Cold chain products.

  • Oral medications, etc.

Inventory of prepositioned stocks

- Make a detailed inventory at least every 6 months of the contents of the different prepositioned stocks.

  • Check the contents of all kits and modules:

  • Check that they are complete and up to date.

  • Change the products at the close expiry date (see prepositioned stocks management guideline).

  • Update the lists and display them

when inventories should be TAKEN?

It is best to do this at the end of the month. However, for organizational reasons, an inventory can be carried out at any time.

For the duration, the following must be taken into account: days for counting and entries, the time needed to find the causes of discrepancies, to correct the discrepancies and to update the stock card.

should inventory be taken just before the order placed?

No, not necessarily, it can be done at the end of the month preceding the order according to the periodicity. In any case, the time between inventory and order preparation must be reduced to a maximum and must not exceed 1 month.

WHAT QUANTITIES IN STOCK SHOULD BE REPORTED ON THE ORDER CALCULATION EXCEL IF THE INVENTORY IS CARRIED OUT THE MONTH BEFORE THE ORDER?

- It is preferable to take the quantities from the inventory. If this is not possible, take the stock of the day and make sure that the lead time and the quantities before expiration are correct.

INVENTory preparation (before)

Inventory is part of the medical inventory management activities and therefore needs to be planned.

  • Respecting the chronogram of orders and the schedule of pharmacy activities; choose the dates of closing of the pharmacy for carrying out the inventory.

  • Take into consideration the time for counting, the time for recounting, research / explanation of discrepancies, and the time for writing the movements on the stock cards and the management software.

-> Do not underestimate and consider only the time for counting.

  • Inform, at least 2 weeks in advance, the persons in charge of the Consumption Units, and the rest of the team that the pharmacy will be closed (no reception of order / preparation / delivery except real emergency) during this (these) day(s).

  • Emergencies must always be able to be treated. The pharmacist or the pharmacy supervisor in collaboration with the project medical manager must analyze the urgency of the request.

-> Inform the medical team of the process to be followed for emergency supplies during the closure of the pharmacy.

  • Mobilize the necessary number of people (pairs).

-> Request and identify additional human resources.

A minimum of 2 people is required to do an inventory: operating in pairs per zone, the pairs must count only the items in their zone and not following the inventory sheet.

For certain items (laboratory, surgery, etc.) it is preferable to call upon specialized human resources (Lab tech, OT nurse, etc.).

  • Tidy up the stock the week before the inventory, note the remaining quantities on the tablet boxes, complete the missing position labels, and identify the different counting zones.

  • Group by expiry date the items in individual packaging (Cannula, syringes, etc.) and group the boxes of reserves by product.

  • Fill in the missing identifications of the cartons / packages.

  • Ensure that expired items have been removed from stock and that those that may expire in the next month are identified.

  • Check that the stock records are up to date: all movements are recorded.

  • Check that all movements are recorded in the management software.

Reconcile the 2 values of theoretical stock: Stock card & management software.

Prepare the necessary small equipment (calculators, pens, tape, markers, scissors, tablet counters, etc.).

Print the inventory sheets from the management software, according to the number of pairs planned.

INVENToRy (during)

  • If there are several pairs (teams of 2 people), assign clearly defined counting area to the different pairs so that no item is left out or the same is counted twice.

  • Each binomial only counts what is in its area; even if an item is scattered / stored in several different areas / locations.

  • The counting is physical: count everything that is found.

  • For products on the shelf, start with the highest level, from left to right, to finish with the last shelf at the bottom right.

  • Collect and count the quantity of product by expiry date; check the order by expiry date.

  • If you find expired products during the count, remove from stock at the day before the inventory, put in the expired zone and fill in the expired notebook.

  • For bulk tablets in large packaging: It is possible to estimate quantities (except for narcotics).

  • Note the result on the inventory sheet and not directly on the stock sheet.

  • Take advantage of the inventory to complete the marking /identification of the boxes with a felt-tip pen -> Write the expiry dates on the boxes with a marker and identify open / opened boxes.

TReaTMENT of the INVENToRy result (after)

  • For the specific procedures for each software refer to the guidelines below:

    • Isystock: see "MSFF_ISYSTOCK_Manual_04-Data Entry» page 11 and " MSFF_ISYSTOCK_Manual_05-Reports " page 8.

    • Unifield: see "Unifield pharmacy tutorial" on page 80.

  • At the end of the count and from the inventory sheets, record the data on the stock card.

  • Identify the items for which there is a difference between physical stock (counted in the inventory) and book inventory (stock card stock, from the management software).

  • Set aside the stock card of the items with discrepancies

  • Investigate the causes of discrepancies.

  • Have another pair count the product.

  • Compare the data recorded in the software with the data on the stock cards.

  • Check if there has been any confusion between similar items (cannula, sutures, etc.): the surplus of one item = missing from another item.

  • Check the calculations on the stock card and correct if errors (it is strictly forbidden to use a corrector or any other method that makes the corrected movement illegible, cross out the movement with an error in one line and then make the corrections next to it or on a new line when it is possible).

  • Check the correspondence with the products and quantities noted in the documents related to the stock movements (packing lists, delivery notes, donation certificates, etc.) to identify whether the discrepancies are related to incomplete or incorrect completion of the stock card or the management into the software.

  • Check whether all stock entries have been noted (forgotten return for example).

  • Check if all stock out movement has been noted (quarantine for example).

  • Correct discrepancies where possible (incomplete or incorrect entries, counting errors) and update the stock card and management software. Corrections of anterior movements are not possible in Unifield.

Remember to note discrepancies in OUT movement in order to take them into account when analyzing AMCs.

  • As the discrepancies are corrected, the causes of the discrepancies should be noted so that corrective measures can be taken afterwards, and help filling the monthly report

  • Inventory count error

  • Data entry errors (Stock card and/or software)

  • Forgotten entry (Stock card and/or software)

  • Other reason identified

  • Undetermined

  • For Unifield users the following types of causes are specific to the tool: Loss; Loss (Scrap); Loss (Sample); Loss (Expiry); Loss (Damage); Discrepancy.

Always choose the cause "discrpancy" and then specify the real cause in the comments.

  • Propose / find solutions to avoid these errors in the future:

  • Specific drawer ("to validate", "to prepare"...) for a better flow of documents.

  • Reinforcement of the double control: Pharmacy: Supervisor AND storekeeper Delivery in unit: storekeeper AND consumption unit supervisor

  • Introduction of a sequential reference to each document.

  • Report:

  • Print a report of discrepancies (table / list of stock differences + explanations) :

  • Have this report signed by the person in charge of the pharmacy and the Project Medical Manager (or Medical Coordinator).

  • Archive this report.

  • In case of discrepancies > or = 30% of the items inventoried, or in case of discrepancies > or = 10% of the stock value, and any discrepancies concerning psychotropic/narcotic drugs and equipment, an incident report must be made.

  • Print the valuated inventory list:

  • Have this list signed by the pharmacy manager and the Project Medical Manager (or Medical Coordinator).

  • Archive the inventory sheets used with the 2 above-mentioned reports.

Communicate the list of available products (including equipment) to the supervisors and the list of products likely to expire to the Project Medical Manager (or Medical Coordinator).
