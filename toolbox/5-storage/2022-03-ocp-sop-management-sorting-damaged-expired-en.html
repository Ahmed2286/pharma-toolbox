Segregation of expired & damaged medical products

At project pharma level:

After recording on the stock card(s), on the expired / damaged register and on the stock management software, separate the products in the expired / damaged area according to the following groups, in order to facilitate the subsequent destruction.

At units level:

After returning to the central pharmacy, recording on the expired / damaged register and the stock management software (at the unit level), separate the products in the expired / damaged area respecting the groups.

Group A Tablets and capsules (DORA, DEXT)
Group B Ampoules, vials and glass bottles (DORA, DINJ, DEXT, DVAC)
Group C Ointment / gel tubes (DEXT, DEXO)
Group D Plastic bottles (DEXT, DORA) and pre-filled syringes (DINJ, DVAC)
Group E Infusions (DINF)
Group F Psychotropic and Narcotic if specific legislation
Group G Anything that is not considered as hazardous product destroyed in the hospital incinerator (SDRE, SINS, SMSU, etc.) = "regular medical waste".
Group H Sharp articles

For hazardous products, such as laboratory reagents, please also respect incompatibilities (do not put in the same carton or near flammable and explosive products for example).

For equipment, please discuss with the biomed focal point.

If any doubt about the classification, please contact ParisPharma.

The expired / damaged register must contain the following information:

  • Code

  • Designation

  • Manufacturer's name

  • Batch number

  • Expiration date

  • Reason for destruction

  • Quantity

To plan destruction, share the list of expired / damaged products to destroy with ParisPharma in order to determine the destruction method for each group.

The list must contain:

  • Code

  • Designation

  • Quantity

Anticipate the destruction in your chronogram of activities (example: destruction of the articles of the group G every 3 months in order to avoid accumulation).
