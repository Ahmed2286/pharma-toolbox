Slide 5: Why do we need a cold chain for drugs?

► Drugs are sensitive to heat: higher is the temperature & duration of exposition, shorter is the shelf life

Also
► Drugs are sensitive to direct sun light

► Drugs are sensitive to cold :
T°C < 0°C = destruction

Slide 6: Vaccine Solvents

¤ Lyophilised vaccines (BCG, Measles, Meningitis, Y. Fever):

SOLVENT must be between +2°C et +8°C for reconstitution (to prevent vaccine shock due to sudden change in temperature)

Must NOT be frozen



Use the adapted diluent for each vaccine (same manufacturer)

Slide 17: Attention

Capacity of ice production mean: a freezer can freeze, for example, 54 icepacks/24h.

NEVER LOAD MORE THAN THE RECOMMANDED CAPACITY

What will happen if you load for example 300 icepacks?

24h latter, the result will be: 300 icepacks cold and not 54 frozen + 246 cold….

Slide 31: Loading procedure for cold box, vaccine carrier & isothermal boxes

1- REMOVE ICEPACKS FROM FREEZER, LET THEM OUTSIDE PROTECTED FROM SUNBEAM UP TO APPARITION OF A BIT OF WATER INSIDE THE ICEPACK

 2- PLACE A PIECE OF CARDBOARD BETWEEN ICEPACKS AND LOAD
 
3- MANDATORY PLACE IN THE MIDDLE OF THE LOAD:

- a cold chain monitor card (3M card)
Attention 3M card must be cooled in a fridge at least 1h00 before
activation


- an irreversible freezing indicator (Freeze-Tag)



- a thermometer recorder Logtag already setted up
