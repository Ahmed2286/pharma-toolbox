100g single use sachet

The packaging for F75/F00 is changing! This change is already being
implemented as the manufacturer had started the production of tins.
This document aims to explain the reasons and consequences of this
change, and give a reminder about Good Hygiene Practices for
preparation (reconstitution,
administration and storage of F75 and
F100.

This change applies for both international purchases and
donations. It is important to check the storage (<30oC) and
transport conditions before accepting a donation. Donations can
only be accepted if the manufacturer is approved by MSF. (check
with your pharma section)

400g tin + scoop included

New ITC codes! F75: NFOSTHMIF7O40

F100: NFOSTHMIF1O40

Why?

1/ For easier reconstitution of F75/F100:

Sachets are supposed to be single use sachet, BUT survey done by
UNICEF, MSF & ACF in Summer 2016 showed:

Preference for F75 and F100 in sachets, but contradictory results
as scoops are currently being used!

=> Single use sachets not used properly.

Very high risk of incorrect dilution with the use of scoop and
sachet, as the reconstitution protocol is not easy to implement.
(18ml of water per scoop, not easy to measure)

Food safety risk as the sachets can not be properly sealed
between uses (risk of contamination of powder)

2/ For better quality of product: use of infant formula
standards for manufacturing of F75/F100

Current supplier can not meet the GMPs (Good Manufacturing
Practices) standards with sachets, only with tins

Good Hygiene Practices
for the reconstitution of F75/F100

•
Mix with a sterilized utensil until completely diluted.

•
Label the recipient: "F75 or F100, day / hour made” (indicate the
product & time of the reconstitution)

•
Cool to feeding temperature (approx.. 37°C).

•
Use within 2 hours.

•
Boil sufficient amount of water for 2 minutes.

•
Pour the exact quantity of water needed (see on the packaging) into
a cleaned and sterilized container (or cup if you prepare just one
cup). Use the graduated measuring jug, 100ml (PCOOMEASHG- -)
to measure the water content. You can let it cool down, but to no less
than 70°C before adding the powder. Use the food grade digital
thermometer (PCOOTHEF-) to check the water temperature.

•
Preparation must take place in a clean environment where counters
have been cleaned and sanitized.

•
Wash your hands using soap and lukewarm water, and dry

them with a clean or disposable cloth.

You must always use the scoop provided in the tin. You must NOT
use scoop from another product (white scoop: F75 / blue scoop: F100)
or manufacturer (Name embossed on the scoop)!

Immediately place scoop directly back into the tin; do not wash it. The
scoop must not be wet: if the scoop gets wet, you must dry it
completely before placing it back into the tin.

• Therapeutic milk must be administrated either at room or body
temperature (37°C). The temperature should be checked with
a food grade thermometer (ex: PCOOTHEF-).

• Therapeutic milk should be served as soon as possible after
preparation and cooling to room or body temperature, and
always within 2hours after its preparation. Reducing the time
from preparation to consumption will reduce the risk of
bacterial contamination (reconstituted milk provides ideal
condition for the growth of harmful bacteria.).

Administration of F75/F100

• Prepare F75/F100 as needed.

• Throw away any feed that has not been consumed within 2 hours.

Conservation of reconstituted F75/F100

It is best to make F75/F100 fresh for each feed and to consume immediately,
as reconstituted F75/F100 provides ideal conditions for the growth of harmful
bacteria. It is why a scoop is provided to prepare as needed.

• Waste management is challenging.

Please share any good ideas of recycling, waste management
with your section nutrition referent!
