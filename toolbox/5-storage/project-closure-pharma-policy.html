PROJECT CLOSURE - PHARMACY

A closure is being planned.
It is necessary to inform the
pharmacist as soon as possible.

The archiving of management

documents must enable the

traceability of movements

related to closure

At the end of the closing procedure:
-
physical stock is at 0.
-
stock cards are at 0
-
stocks in Isystock are at 0
-
Updated backup is saved in the
Sharek space

As soon as the closure date is known:


Set up a closure schedule for the pharmacy.

Calculate your needs until closure and list any excess quantities in stock.

Discuss the list of beneficiaries for possible donations with the CoMed.

Review the situation with the biomedical manager.

Review the completeness of management documents and archives.

If activities are being transferred, share consumption data to ensure the continuity of treatments that must not be interrupted.

Contact Paris Pharma to prepare the annual report.

Organize electronic and paper files: ensure that documents are filed in the correct order and properly identified.

One month before closing: a complete physical inventory is mandatory (see the “OCP MEMO Guide Inventories»)

EXCESS STOCK

It may be decided to transfer part or all the stock in coordination. In this case:

Separate different stocks

if necessary (central
stock, pre-positioned,
expired, damaged, etc.)

Clearly label the boxes

and prepare the
detailed packing lists.

Prepare the boxes in
accordance with health

and safety regulations.

Ensure storage capacity in

coordination, particularly

for cold chain products.

Communicate with

the logistics
(transportation) and

finance (cash
request) teams.

Donation certificates are legal documents. They must be correctly filled
out (expiration date, batch number) and signed by the MedRef.

MOVEMENT ENCODING IN ISYSTOCK
At project level: movement OUT 42-Unplanned donation to coordination.
If you are sending expired items to the coordination for destruction, be sure that they have been previously recorded with OUT 21-Expired
movement.

At coordination level : movement IN 4- Donation from project.
The coordination team must integrate all donated items into its day-to-day management (Isystock and stock cards).
Receipt of expired items for destruction is not a donation and must not be entered into Isystock or the stock cards.

OTHER DONATIONS
The Comed is responsible for deciding which donations are made to which beneficiaries (other OCP projects, other departments, the Ministry of
Health, other NGOs, etc.), in accordance with protocols and regulations, considering the capacity for use and consumption of the partners.
Beware of items with a shelf life of less than 6 months!
For equipment, ensure that spare parts and consumables are available in the country.

SENDING OUTSIDE THE COUNTRY

Never leave the return of medical items with IMS (International Mobile Staff).

Decisions made on a case-by-case basis for expensive equipment and materials, with involvement of the Cell and the Medical and Supply Depts.

EXPIRED & DAMAGED
If possible, expired and damaged stock should be destroyed at project level before closure, following MSF procedures and complying with local
regulations. If this isn't possible, the stock should be sent to the coordination, after checking local regulations on the transport of expired and
damaged products.

DOCUMENTS AND ARCHIVING
The following documents must be sent to coordination or HQ, depending on whether MSF remains in the country, and archived for the period
specified in the “Pharma Retention table” available in the Toolbox_Records_Management_and_Archiving.aspx

-
Stock cards sorted by item code
-
Donation certificate folder
-
Documents related to INs, OUTs, and inventories

The latest Isystock backup must be saved on Sharek, in the project's “1031 Isystock Back-Ups” folder, with all stock units (main, intermediate,
special) set to zero following donations or destruction of expired items (see previous page for types of movements).
