Control at reception: RECEIPT & QUALITY CHECK

  1. Receipt

If your response to any of the following questions is affirmative,

  1. the product should be quarantined and

  2. the supplier or transporter informed and corrective action recommended

  3. the problem recorded in the supplier Follow up Card

If necessary, a product claim form together with photos is sent to the section pharmacist and ESC if it concerns international procurement

  1. Initial Receipt

  • Wrong or missing way bill and packing list from distributor.

  • Wet, open or broken boxes?

  • Wrong quantity supplied?

  • Cold chain not maintained?

  • Temperature monitoring devices for non cold chain items shows excursion of temperature

In case of Local purchase, additional checks are necessary

  • Right product

  • Price difference with the pro forma?

  • Right manufacturer and manufacturing site/address as per order

  • Acceptable expiry date?

    1. Final Receipt

Labels

  • Manufacturer or expiry date different from the ones on the pro forma?

  • Information on label lacking, illegible or wrong?

Packaging

  • Wrong quantity of units per packing?

  • Containers or packaging not sealed?

  • Products not protected from humidity (containers not tightly closed) and/or light (transparent containers, ampoules, vials, etc.)?

  • Cracked or broken packaging?

  • Spillage of product on the outside of bottles or vials?

  1. Quality check: Sign of degradation per pharmaceutical forms or products

If your response to any of the following questions is affirmative,

  1. the product should be quarantined and

  2. the problem should be reported

  • in case of international procurement: both ESC and SP should be informed and the product claim form should be filled up

  • in case of local procurement: the SP and if available the international pharmacist should be informed and the product claim form should be filled up

Tablets

  • Tablets that are soft and stick together?

  • Small black spots, blotches, foreign material, traces of mould?

  • Unusual smell?

  • Cracks, uneven edges or coating?

  • Differences in colour, shape or size between the tablets?

  • Broken tablets in blisters, ≥1% broken tablets in bottle?

  • Tablets are broken or powdery?

Capsules

  • Capsule shells are wet (when affected by humidity the capsules stick together)?

  • Damaged capsules so that powder comes out of the capsules?

  • Discoloration or smudging of the shell?

  • Differences in color between the capsules?

Solutions / syrups

  • Turbid, lumps, flocks, particles, traces of mould or decay?

  • Color differences between the contents of the bottles?

  • Unusual smell or too viscous to pour?

Vaginal tablets

  • Discoloration?

  • Color differences between them?

  • Particles stuck to the surface?

Ointments / creams / pastes

  • Inhomogeneous consistency (check a small amount between 2 glass plates)?

  • Inhomogeneous colour?

  • Unusual smell?

  • Traces of mould or decay?

Vials

  • Presence of particles in the solution?

  • Color differences between units of same product?

Powder for reconstitution (for injections or syrups)

  • Presence of brown or black dots in the powder?

  • Is the powder affected by humidity (lumps instead of free flowing powder)?

  • After reconstitution: not all the dry powder has completely dissolved?

  • After reconstitution: is not formed a homogenous suspension (turbid, milk-like, without lumps) or a clear solution?

Intravenous large volume infusions

  • Presence of particles in the solution?

  • Small holes at closures and seams (if so, sterility is lost)?

  • Color differences between bottles, bags?

Sterile medical material and sutures

  • Material is supplied in a well closed box? Loose unprotected units?

  • Small holes in primary packaging?

  • Dust on the outside of primary packaging?

  • Dust or dirt within primary packaging (only visible when packaging is transparent)?

In Vitro Devices

  • Test kits complete?

  • Cold chain maintained?

  • Containers or packaging well sealed? Boxes are not torn, wet or damaged?

  • Packed in pouches/packaging protecting tests from humidity and light?

  • Instructions for use in box?
