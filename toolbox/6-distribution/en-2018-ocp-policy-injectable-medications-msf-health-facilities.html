Injectable medications in MSF health facilities

The safe administration of an injectable medicine is:

One patient / one syringe / one needle/ one time

Ampoules, vials and bags must normally be used for one patient only.

There are 2 main concerns:

1.
Chemical stability: once reconstituted or open to the atmosphere,
medications can deteriorate rapidly.
2.
Infection prevention: apart from multi-dose vials of vaccines, injectable
medicines do not contain any preservative, presenting a risk of
microbiological contamination and spread of infection.

General principles:

• DON’T insert needle/syringe already used with a patient into an ampoule/vial/bag.
• DON’T use the same needle or syringe with different patients.
• Even if the needle has been changed, one syringe has to be used only for one
patient
• Injections should always be prepared and administered by the same person.
• Injectable medicines should be administered immediately after preparation.
• Vials/bags should ALWAYS be discarded whenever sterility is compromised or
questionable.



Unfinished vials or ampoules should be returned to pharmacy for appropriate disposal (to
prevent any illegitimate use and environmental risks among others).

In case of controlled drugs, this return should be written in the controlled
drugs register (unit and pharmacy).
Adjust to the country legislation regarding return of empty vials to the
pharmacy.

Ampoules:

• SINGLE USE ONLY: use for one patient then immediately discards the ampoule (and
if there is any remaining content, the ampoule should be returned to the pharmacy).
There is no exception to this rule.
• DON’T keep an open ampoule covered with a piece of tape neither with a piece of
medicated gauze.
• DON’T use a syringe as storage for a medicine from an ampoule as the materials of
the syringe may react with medicines.

Vials:

• For inpatients: the use of one vial for multiple patients is only allowed during the
same medication round for injectable medicines given in small doses, administered
always by the same person, as it is the case in pediatric/neonatology wards.
 Because of the increased risk of contamination, this practice should be avoided
in others wards (ER, OT, ICU, OPD, dressing room, etc.).
• Before each use, thoroughly disinfect the rubber top of the vial as per MSF Nursing
Care Guidelines, before inserting the needle for each use. Use a new syringe and
needle for each patient.
• DISCARD the needle in the rubber top after preparation for each patient.
• DON’T store reconstituted vials.
• After the patient round, discard the vial (and if there is any remaining content, the
ampoule should be returned to the pharmacy).

Large volume IV fluids:


For Flexible plastic bags but NOT Nipple Head Bottles & EUROHEAD bottles

• The use of one IV bag for multiple patients is only allowed during the same
medication round where very small volumes are required (e.g. neonatology).
• Accessing bags multiple times via the gummi/needle entrance point is not
recommended since this can lead to leakage and increased risk of contamination.
• In the event that a bag needs to be accessed multiple times, to ensure best
practice there is a spike specifically designed to allow repeated access to IV-bags
and fluid bottles: SINSTRFDS2- DISPENSING SPIKE, non-vented, 2-W valve+
needleless-connect.

• Before each use, thoroughly disinfect the access point as per MSF Infection Control
Guideline, before inserting the needle/connecting the syringe.
• Discard the needle after withdrawing the fluid from the bag.
• Always ensure that a new needle and syringe is used EVERY time.
• DON’T store used bag after the medication round.
• After the medication round, discard the bag (and any remaining liquid).

The implementation of this guideline will have a positive impact on the quality of care but it
will also have an impact on budget and consumption. Implementation must be planned with
the medical, coordination teams as well as the HQ’s cell.
Additional quantity could be ordered if necessary, and FMCs (Forecasted Monthly
Consumptions) should be adjusted accordingly.

MSF OCP RECOMMENDATIONS CONCERNING ANESTHETIC DRUGS

All ampoules and vials for use by anesthesia personnel are for single-patient use only.

In certain contexts, separation of medications into aliquots from a single-use vial or
ampoule has been permitted due to restrictions on access to or availability of critical
medications, but requires a specific protocol for each medication that has been
previously validated by the pharmacy and anesthesia referents in the Medical
Department.

Emergency drugs that have been prepared beforehand do not have to be discarded after
each surgical case unless the syringe was used.

All pre-prepared emergency medications are to be discarded at the end of the surgical
day without exception.

Propofol must be discarded after 6 hours when it is drawn up into a syringe.

All open vials/medications in the OT (Operating Theater) must be labelled with the
name of the drug, the concentration, the date and time the vial was first opened or
time of preparation, and name of the person who opened it or the initials of the
provider preparing the medication.

Use preprinted stickers available in the ITC Catalogue.

If the vial is not labelled with the information on its first opening, it cannot be used
and must be discarded.

WHO RECOMMENDATIONS CONCERNING MULTI-DOSE VIALS POLICY FOR VACCINES

http://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/10665/135972/1/WHO_IVB_14.07_eng.pdf?ua=1

SUMMARY OF WHO MULTI-DOSE VIAL POLICY:

“All opened WHO-prequalified multi-dose vials of vaccines should be discarded at the end
of the immunization session, or within six hours of opening, whichever comes first, UNLESS
the vaccine meets all four of the criteria listed below. If the vaccine meets the four criteria,
the opened vial can be kept and used for up to 28 days after opening. The criteria are as
follows.

1. The vaccine is currently prequalified by WHO.
2. The vaccine is approved for use for up to 28 days after opening the vial, as
determined by WHO.

3. The expiry date of the vaccine has not passed.
4. The vaccine vial has been, and will continue to be, stored at WHO- or
manufacturer recommended temperatures; furthermore, the vaccine vial monitor, if one is
attached, is visible on the vaccine label and is not past its discard point, and the vaccine has
not been damaged by freezing.”

The term "multi-dose vial policy" refers to a vial of vaccine from which one or more doses
have been used during an immunization session and that can be reused during 28 days.

The WHO recommendations apply only to vaccines which meet the WHO specifications
concerning efficiency and thermo-stability, and which contain an adequate quantity of
preserving agent. All vaccines provided by MSF meet these requirements.

LIQUID vaccines* (oral polio, DTP, TT, DT, Td, hepatitis B and liquid formulations of Hib or
pentavalent (DTP Hib Hep B) vaccine) may be used 28 days after opening the vial provided
that ALL of the following conditions are met:

 The expiry date has not passed.
 The vaccines have been stored under appropriate cold chain conditions (+2 to +8° C).
Storage conditions have been monitored (freeze tag, logtag).
 Aseptic technique has been used to withdraw all doses:

o New sterile syringe and needle for each injection; needle should not be left
inside the vial.
o The septum of the vial has not been submerged in water.
 The vaccine vial monitor (VVM), if attached, still indicates that the vaccine can be
used.

*Be careful: If the VVM is attached in another location than on the label (e.g. cap or neck
of the ampoule) the multi-doses open vial policy is not applicable (ex: PCV vaccine).

 They must be discarded in the following cases:

 Procedures of asepsis were not rigorously respected.
 Cold chain was broken.
 Open vial is suspected to be contaminated.
 There is evidence of contamination (change of aspect, particles in suspension,
etc.).
 VVM inner square is the same color or darker than the outside circle.

Reconstituted FREEZE-DRIED vaccines (measles, yellow fever, BCG, meningitis) must be
discarded at the end of the immunization session or within 6 hours of opening, whichever
comes first.

In case of doubt if the Multi-Dose vial policy is applicable check WHO prequalification
website:
http://www.who.int/immunization_standards/vaccine_quality/PQ_vaccine_list_en/en/
