Slide 2: Objective:

This Memo is for mission pharmas/ PharmaCos to explain how to get accurate and up to date backorder’s report from MSF Logistique (MSFL) portal, by explaining:
The order line’s cycle
The different types of stocks involved in the order’s reception in OCP’s missions
The ways to manage the cargos’ reception depending on the type of stock

Introduction:

The backorders are an essential part in the stock management, forecasting and quantification of the needs and it plays an important role in the budget planning and follow-up of the project / mission.
The pharma/ Supply team usually follows the international orders via MSF Logistique's portal identifying the expected pipelines for the mission.

Slide 3: Stock Location Types:

The reception of the international medical order depends on the context of the mission. In general there are two different types of stock locations in OCP involved in the reception of international cargos.

Detailed Stock: usually in the project, or at the coordination level, as a central Stock allocated where the coordination acts as rear base and contains part of or all of the stock of a project.
Transit Stock: where the cargo is stored temporarily in the warehouse, for less than one month*, awaiting its dispatch to the final destination without opening the boxes but following the expiry dates; the stock is managed by excel sheet.
*If the cargo or parts of it remains in the transit stock of the coordination for more than 1 month, the coordination should treat the remaining stock of this cargo the same way as a detailed stock.

Slide 5: Order’s Line Status

Each order sent to MSF Logistique goes through this cycle and each order line has one of the following statuses:

Confirmed: The order is validated and it is being processed by MSFL

Ready To Ship (RTS): The order line is packed and Ready To Ship at MSFL, waiting for the greenlight from the mission to be dispatched.

Shipped: The order line has been shipped from MSFL and is on the way to the mission.

Arrived: The shipment containing the order line arrived in the mission and is waiting for the physical confirmation of the goods’ reception.

Received: The Mission checked and confirmed the physical reception of the order line.

Closed: All the quantities belonging to this order line have been received in the mission.

Cancelled: The order has been cancelled and it will not be processed by MSF Logistique.

Slide 6: Cargo’s Reception:

Upon reception of an international cargo, the mission must confirm the reception through two different steps.

Step 1: Transport Reception
In this step the mission confirms the reception of all the parcels included in the cargo manifest.
Confirming the parcels’ reception changes the status of the order line from “Shipped” to “Arrived” while the arrived quantities of the order line will STILL show in the backorders balance and under the column “Quantity shipped not yet arrived” until the goods’ reception is done.
This step is handled by the supply team and usually the confirmation happens once the cargo is received at the warehouse depending on the context of the mission.

Slide 7: Step 2: Goods Reception

In this step, there is a physical check of the parcels to confirm that they contain all the items that are mentioned in the detailed packing list of the cargo and that there is no issue with the quality of the received goods.
The physical confirmation of the content of the parcels is handled by the pharmacy team for the medical orders.
The pharma team communicates with the supply team who will confirm the goods reception of the cargo in the portal.

Once this step is completed, the status of the order line will be switched in the report from “arrived” to “received” and the quantities will be removed from the backorders balance.

PS: In case the cargo is stored in the transit stock at the coordination’s level for more than one month, the process of the detailed centralised allocated stock has to be followed for the remaining stock belonging to the project at the coordination.

Slide 8: How to get Backorder’s Report

Orders’ follow-up report:
This report compiles the total backorders lines with different statuses.
To find the actual quantities in the backorders you can follow these steps:
Step 1
Extract and open the Orders shipment follow up report from the portal.
From the drop list on MSFlog Portal >> Orders >> Order’s follow-up
In the new opened page, choose >> Your mission
The type of the order >> Medical
Year >> on going.
Then extract the excel file by clicking on the backorders follow-up button.

Slide 12: For The TSR Report

Order shipment follow-up report:
This report compiles the different cargos with detailed information of the contents of the cargos and their different statuses (RTS, Shipped, Received) with the estimated date of arrival of the RTS and shipped cargos.
Compiling the information of this report with the order follow-up report will allow having the ETA of each cargo line for the RTS and Shipped lines.

To do this you need to follow the below steps.

The previous report can be used for the TSR as well, however, the ETA of all the pipelines will be the same as the estimated date of reception of the IO.

For a more detailed analysis and to include the pipelines with their estimated time of arrival of each different cargo in the stock status you need to compile the information from two different reports (Order follow-up report and order shipment follow up report).

Slide 18: In the backorders sheet

You will find all the backorders for all the projects in your mission with ETA for the shipped and RTS backorders while the confirmed ones will be blank.
You can filter the backorders of the selected project, copy the filtered data to be filled in the TSR under the corresponding columns.
In the TSR, the ETA of the blank cells will be the beginning of the next month of the ETA of next IO in the settings sheet.
The lines with ETA will be included in the stock status of the project at the beginning of the next month of their ETA.

Slide 19: Key Messages

Medical cargos already received in the mission should not be treated as part of backorders, regardless of stock location.
The Pharmacy team in the coordination should ALWAYS check the cargos with “arrived” status and check if the quantities are recorded in the project or coordination stock (either in Isystock or excel sheet).
Upon confirmation of the arrived cargos, a prompt physical inspection of box contents is required, followed by goods reception on the portal.
Conduct a physical inspection of the remaining goods in transit stock if the cargo or any parts of it remain in transit for more than 1 month.
Goods' reception on the portal should occur immediately after the physical inspection of the parcels’ contents, to update quantities and remove them from the backorder report.
