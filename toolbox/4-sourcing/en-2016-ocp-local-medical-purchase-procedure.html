LOCAL MEDICAL PURCHASE PROCEDURE

Regarding drugs and medical devices for OCP missions

INTRODUCTION

Local medical purchase = exception

MSF policy for medical supply gives clear priority to imports from the central logistics centers located in
Europe: MSF Logistique, MSF Supply and Amsterdam Procurement Unit.

Exceptions for local purchase should be evaluated by country (eg in case of legal or administrative barriers to
imports) and on a case by case basis (emergency ...). If the decision to use local purchase is approved with an
operational perspective and by the Medical Director, it must be done following the recommendations made
by a section pharmacist after the assessment of the local market.

International procurement versus local purchase

It is important to recognize that from a quality point of view, validation of sources for local purchase can-not
be compared to the validation performed for the MSF purchase centers. For several reasons (not
exhaustive):
-
Most markets in countries where MSF works are risky, with little to no regulation by supervisory
authorities.
-
The time spent by pharmacists on visits of producers for local purchase (4 to 5 hours per site) is
much lower than for audits for international validation (1-2 days / site).
-
A portfolio assessment for each product is not possible for drugs purchased locally.
-
In countries where import is not possible means that compromises must be made, as there is no
other choice but to approve local sources even though none might meet quality standards.

Therefore, we can say that the validation of products for local purchase is often made on the basis of a
risk-benefit assessment in relation to a particular context, while products validated through the
international MSF purchase centers are made with a full validation and comply with international quality
standards.

It is important to bear in mind that there is generally no system of pharmacovigilance in the countries where
MSF works. Therefore, the absence of side effect or treatment failure reporting is not proof of drug quality.

Reasons that do not justify local procurement

To support local markets is not a "mandate" or a desire of MSF. To prefer local purchases for this reason, by
compromising on the quality of medicines, is equivalent to exposing patients to unnecessary risks, which is
not ethically acceptable.

The price is not a valid criterion for choosing a local supply while supply from a MSF purchase center is
possible. Quality comes before the price.

Accepting donations of non-validated sources (or even black-listed) from Ministry of Health or any partner,
when supply of sources with higher quality is possible (MSF supply center) is not recommended.

NB : any donation from a partner must be accompanied by a request a for quality validation from the
referent pharmacist in HQ (see below)

SITUATIONS WHEN LOCAL MEDICAL PRUCHASE IS JUSTFIED

There are two types of situations justifying a local medical purchase:

1. Legal or administrative barriers to importation:
These import barriers may involve some or all of the products used on the mission (for example barriers to
only narcotic and psychotropic drugs). Some countries in which MSF is working impose stringent legal or
administrative barriers to importation (total impossibility of importation in 10 out of 32 in 2015). In this case
a local purchase procedure is established for regular supply of a list of products to be purchased from
approved local distributors of MSF-qualified manufacturers.

2. Medical Emergency :
This constitutes urgent and essential needs for patients in a country where the importation is theoretically
possible and where the mission often buys from MSF Logistic. This is known as an exceptional local
procurement process which is the responsibility of the Medical Coordinator (Medco) and requires
authorization from headquarters.

PRINCIPES DE LES OF VALIDATION

Reminder...
Supplier/wholesaler/distributor: commercial establishment for the public or private sale of
pharmaceuticals.
Manufacturer/producer: laboratory that manufactures pharmaceuticals


In each country where local purchase is regular or is exceptionally authorized by the Medical Director, a list
of suppliers and manufacturers has been validated during a visit done by one of the five pharmacists of MSF
operational centers. This list is common to all sections working in the same country and is confidential.

A supplier is validated on criteria for adherence to good practices for storage and supply system.

A manufacturer is validated on criteria for compliance in good manufacturing practices. That producer may
be local (validation by the section pharmacist during his visit) or international (validation by the network of
pharmacists MSF). A manufacturer is not validated for all products it offers. Validation is done product by
product and for each manufacturing site (a manufacturer may have several manufacturing sites worldwide,
of variable quality). We therefore validate only for the product + manufacturer pair for a given site.

A product can be purchased only if the supplier and the manufacturer are validated

Countries where local purchase is the only option (countries are 100% local supply), databases have been
implemented, which compile and gather all information related to pharmaceutical local market in the
country.
These databases can only be updated by the section pharmacist in HQ who do so regularly (can be
intersectional pharmacist based in the country /region).

STEPS FOR LOCAL PURCHASE

STEP 1 : Expression and validation of needs

For the expression of the needs that will need local purchase, the circuit is the same as for an international
order : defined by the project team and validated by MedCo.

The order form is the reference tool for expressing the needs of the project for medical products for local
purchase. It contains all necessary information for the PharmaCo and MedCo to determine needs and Cellule
Medical Manager to confirm the order.

Whenever possible, it’s recommended to keep the name and supply codes of MSF products. Creation of new
codes for specific products could be considered in case of regular local purchases (importation impossible)

MedCo/PharmaCo submit the order form/validation source sheet with the quantities requested and price to
the Cellule Medical Manager.
In case of regular local purchases, reference prices are based on the last purchases (prices are updated in
Isystock after each reception). This is to estimate the cost of the order.

The Cellule Medical Manager validates the order from an operational point of view, and validates a decision
to switch to local purchase in case of emergency.

The medical order must follow the process of validation of the mission which can require specific signatures
based on the proposed cost (FinCo and Hom)

STEP 2 : proforma request from a validated supplier

Based on the list of suppliers validated by the visit of a pharmacist HQ referent, the PharmaCo (or MedCo)
request a proforma with the supplier identified and validated, in order to collect following information :
Price
Manufacturer : name
Location site-address- (different than address of HQ)
Expiration date
Expected delivery date

Template of proforma for medical local purchase- (annex2)

If there is no recommendation available on the mission regarding validated suppliers, please contact the
referent pharmacist in HQ

Step 3 : Validation of the order by the Pharmacist Referent in HQ (Quality)

The validation of the pharmacist referent must be systematically requested by the MedCo or PharmaCo in
the following cases :

- Exceptional local purchase in a country where international procurement is possible.
- Local purchase of a product that was not included in the database of validated manufacturers
(i.e a new product or product with a non-validated source) in a country using local procurement
exclusively
- Opening of a new emergency project in a new country ( give priority to international
procurement in emergency if possible)

The pharmacist referent fills the validation form, and replies to the CoMed/Copharma, prioritizing choice
according quality criteria.

At “quality” criteria equivalent, copharma (or CoMed) could choose the best supplier for each product
according the delivery time, availability, and price.

STEP 4 : Order at supplier(s)

After validation by the Cellule Medical Manager, pharmacist referent, ad greenlight of coordination, the
CoPharma (or Comed) (+/- logistic department) orders from the supplier(s) using the “local medical purchase
order form”(annex 3). One order form should be filled for each supplier. The financial coordinator has to be
consulted for the procedure and terms of payment to the supplier.

The order form specifies quantities requested, price, manufacturer name, and delivery date (based on
information from the pro forma).

In case of regular and large volume orders, the copharma (comed) with the logistic department could
negotiate prices with suppliers, and eventually sign a contract.

STEP 5 : Follow up and reception

A duplicate of the order must be kept at the pharmacy to track the order.

The control at reception requires following the SOP “Control at reception” (annex 4), and compliance with
information mentioned in order form (product, quantity, supplier, manufacturer, expired date, batch
number, price)

Information regarding the batch number, expiration date and invoice number are completed upon receipt of
products to ensure traceability of orders.

The capital pharmacist or the project pharmacy manager manages the reception, the order follow-up and
the update of prices in Isystock when receiving the product. He validates the invoice and gives the
information to the financial coordinator for the payment of the bill.
