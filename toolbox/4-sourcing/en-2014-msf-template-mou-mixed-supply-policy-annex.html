Sample Memorandum of Understand (MoU) for

MSF – MOH Mixed Supply Project

  1. Introduction and Purpose

This memorandum of understanding (MoU) is between the Ministry of Health (MoU) and Medecins sans Frontieres (MSF) to establish a system for the shared and sustained supply of quality medical products in the MOH – MSF project.

  2. Quality Assurance Principles (selection, procurement, distribution)

    1. Selection of medical products for the mixed supply shall be based on the National Essential Medicines List (EML) or the WHO Model EML, with reference to the context of the MOH-MSF project. For each medical product, the supply must be defined as “from MOH” or “from MSF.” In cases of MOH supply (a) shortages or (b) quality problems (whether actual quality problem reported, or if the MOH supply is not in accordance with the quality criteria outlined in 4.2 below), MSF may “buffer” the MOH supply, defined on a product-by-product basis, for an agreed-upon duration.

    2. International norms for the procurement of medical products shall be followed by MOH and MSF, while respecting national medicines regulation.

Mixed supply shall be accepted for medical products procured by MOH (or for MOH e.g. via donations or via third party procurement mechanisms) using the following criteria:

  1. WHO Prequalification List / selected other pre-qualified manufacturers with WHO GMP certification (linked to specific manufacturing site and product specifications)

  2. USFDA approval

  3. Marketing authorization from a Stringent Regulatory Authority (SRA) with registration in a Highly Regulated Country (HRC)

In turn, the MOH commits to facilitate the importation of medical products by MSF from MSF procurement centres by allowing medical imports while respecting national regulation and rules, based on MSF’s recognized QA scheme.

  1. Good Distribution Practices (GDP) shall be based on the WHO Model QA System for Procurement Agencies, while respecting national regulatory guidelines for GDP.

  1. Define the terms for mixed supply

    1. sharing of sources information before each supply from MOH, including collaboration on National Procurement Forecasting exercises

    2. accountability along the distribution chain (planning and management of transport, distribution, storage, dispensing)

    3. responsibilities for traceability / notification in case of quality problem

    4. timing for deliveries

    5. responsibilities in case of stock-out of MOH or MSF supply
