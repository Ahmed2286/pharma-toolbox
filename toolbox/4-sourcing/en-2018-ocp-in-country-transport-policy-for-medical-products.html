Introduction

High and low temperature and humidity exposure (Relative Humidity % >65%) increase the degradation speed of
medical products. Possible consequences might turn into reduction of their efficacy and length life, appearance of
potential toxic metabolites and consequently impact on safety.

This policy defines how to mitigate the main risk factors of extreme temperatures and humidity exposure such as :
inadequately equipped vehicles, long distances, outsourcing of transportation, unclear roles and responsibilities
between the different departments, adverse climate conditions, higher transportation frequency (eg : emergency
orders, loans, donations), lack of temperature monitoring & control and lack of traceability.

The 2012 MSF Policy on International Transportation of Pharmaceutical doesn’t cover the transportation at mission
level and doesn’t detail regarding the different transportation means.

Roles and responsibility

The Medical Director and the Section Pharmacist set the norms for the quality of medical products and this include
ensuring compliance with the storage and transport conditions required by the manufacturers.

The Supply Department ensures that in the mission the correct transport conditions are respected, the appropriate
means of transportation are provided and regular temperatures monitoring is performed during transport.

In case of temperature excursions, the Section Pharmacist/ParisPharma must be contacted for technical advice.

In case of relevant deviations the Medical Director takes the final decision regarding the validation of the use of the
product or its destruction.

At mission level, the pharmacist1, as GDP (Good Distribution Practices) responsible person, is in charge of checking
the presence of appropriate temperature monitoring tools and collecting the temperature data from transportation
in collaboration with the supply team, in order to check the compliance with GDP. The pharmacist is in charge of
reporting any excursions of temperature to HQ Section pharmacist/ParisPharma.

Concerned families

•
D category, N category : all medical products « controlled room temperature » : DORA, DINJ, DINF, DEXT,
DEXO, NFOS
•
S category, E category : products labelled « to be stored under 25°C or 30°C » : SSTD, SLAS, SINS, SSUT,
SCTD, SDRE, EEDM
•
Not concerned by this policy : products requiring « storage between2°C - 8°C » as they should be transported
as cold chain items : DINJ, DVAC, SSDT , ELAE...

General principle

As par pharmaceutical norms, medical products must be stored and transported according to manufacturer’s
specifications based on the stability studies performed by the manufacturer, and described in the labelling of the
product or the secondary packaging. These specifications for controlled room temperature products are <25°C or <
30°C.

Each shipment should be monitored with a temperature monitoring device (log Tag). For countries with negative
temperature during winter, a Freeze Tag should be also used.

For in-country transportation of medical products, excursion of temperature is tolerated for short period of
time during transportation (below 35°C for max 1 hour).

ALERT POINT :

o Excursions > 30° C and for more than 1 hour.
o Excursion < 2° C whatever the duration

Inform ParisPharma + Supply-Par

ACTION POINT:

o Excursion > 40°C whatever the duration
o Excursion < 0°C whatever the duration

Report to the Section Pharmacist/Parispharma + Supply-Par
Affected items should be put into quarantine

Good Distribution Practices -Transport

General

•
When controlled temperature transportation system is possible and affordable, the mission should prefer
this mean of transport.
•
Exposure to sunlight, heat or extreme cold, and rain outside the pharmacy should be avoided.
•
The medical products should be transported without others products that can potentially contaminate them
such as fuel, cement dust, fresh food or chemical products.
•
In case transportation is outsourced a clear agreement should be signed. This includes expectation of
compliance with the norms for transportation as described in this document.
•
Upon arrival, the medical products should be stored as soon as possible in the pharmacy under the
controlled storage conditions.

Trucks/cars/plane

•
Canvas trucks and open pick ups should be avoided and hardtop truck should be preferred.
•
The general aspect of the truck should be checked (absence of fissure, perforation or holes).
•
The absence of humidity and moisture should be also checked.
•
The cleanliness of the truck before loading should be checked (absence of litter, dust, food).
•
The truck should have a locking system to avoid any violation of the parcels during the transportation.
•
The loading height of the truck should be respected and the parcels (on pallets) should not be in direct
contact with the wall or the roof.
•
The shipments should not be exposed to direct sunlight.
•
White cartoon boxes should be preferred to any other colored boxes to protect from an eventual sunlight
exposure.
•
In case air transport is chosen, the exposure of the parcels to sunlight, heat or extreme cold and rain on the
tarmac/track should be limited to the minimum.
