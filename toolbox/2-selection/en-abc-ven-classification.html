All articles in stock don’t have the same importance:


Items with fast turnover (stock rotation),

Items with low turnover,

Value can differ from one item to another.

ABC method = popular technique to understand and
categorize inventories / articles.

Allows to:
Identify the most important articles (generally a small number of

items),
Pay more attention on these items.

« Prioritization tool »

WHAT IS ABC ANALYSIS?

Département Médical
5

ABC method aims to sort the items of a stock in 3 groups:


Class A : products of a high importance / cost

Very tight control & tracking

Class B : products of a middle importance / cost

Tight control & moderate tracking

Class C : products of a low importance / cost

Little control & tracking

More appropriate for stocks with a lot of articles.

Prerequisites :


Data must be reliable.

Unit prices updated.

WHAT IS ABC ANALYSIS?

Département Médical
6

Can be performed on several parameters:


Value of outgoings (consumption or sales) during a period:

= Articles the most important considering the purpose of
a stock which is to satisfy the needs.


Value of stock articles at some point:

= Analysis allows to know what we have in stock and
appreciate the stock management.
Can be performed only on a part of the stocked articles:
not moving stocks, consumables, etc.


Value of procurement or other entries in stock during a period.

Parameter will be chosen according to the objective of
the analysis.

So, the ABC analysis helps us to:

To classify the items in 3 categories (A, B and C) according to their
importance / volume (in financial value or consumption).

The ABC analysis is used to:

Prioritize the stock management,
Evaluate the category of each item (ABC) with its operational meaning

/ impact (VEN),
Elaborate and assess the medical orders and budgets.

VEN ANALYSIS

VEN ANALYSIS

Département Médical
42

VEN analysis is a well-known method to help set up priorities for
purchasing medicines and for keeping / managing stock.

Articles are divided in 3 categories according to their health impact:

V = Vital
E = Essential
N = Non-essential

VEN analysis allows medicines of differing efficacy and usefulness to be
compared (difference with ABC and therapeutic category analyses).

INTRODUCTION

Département Médical
43

Vital drugs (V):


Potentially life-saving or crucial to provide basic health services.

Essential drugs (E):


Effective for less severe but significant forms of disease, but not
necessarily vital to provide basic health care.

Non-essential drugs (N):

•
Used for minor or self-limited illnesses, the less important items in stock.

If ABC analysis helps us to:


Classify the items in 3 categories (A, B and C) according to their volume
(in financial value or consumption),

VEN analysis helps us to:

Classify the articles according their operational impact / meaning (health

impact).

That is helpful when :


You have to prioritize the stock management and select items that you
have to follow closely (sentinel system),

You have to analyze your stock (risk of shortages for instance),

You have to prepare a medical order,

You have to prepare a budget, etc.

Use Vlook up when you will do stock analysis, prepare budget, etc. !!!
