MSL

DEFINITION
List of medical articles:
- is needed for the correct running of a medical
activity / project.
- has to be validated for use and procurement
(without any notion of quantity).
- should contain both consumable and not
consumable medical articles, which should be
available for the project.

OBJECTIVES / RATIONALE
- Ensure a rationalized use of medical articles.
- Ensure coherence between medical activities and the
articles used.
- Ensure coherence with MSF guidelines and evidence
based protocols.
- Ensure coherence across projects.
- Simplify the process for medical orders.
- Support more efficient supply.
- Improve stock management.

ACCOUNTABILITY
- At Project level: Project Medical
Referent.
- At Coordination level: Medical
Coordinator.
- At Headquarter level: Cell Medical
responsible.

 International Non-Proprietary name (INN) must be used referring to

pharmaceutical substances or active pharmaceutical ingredients.

- Each INN is a unique name that is globally recognized and is public
property.
- Beside the INN, each article is defined by a unique “Code” by MSF ITC
(International Technical Coordination):

1 Medical Article = 1 Code

 A Standard (STD) article is an article that has been validated by all Operational

Centers (OC) and is present in the ITC Catalogue / Summary of Changes.

 A Non Standard (NST) article is an article validated only by some OCs and not

appearing on the ITC Catalogue.

 The MSF OCP Medical Standard List: articles validated by Medical Director, with

or without restrictions.

 It is essential for the project to:

o Use the last update of the Isystock database1.
o Update Isystock prices and codes at each order reception.
o Refer to the most updated version of:

-
ITC Catalogues.
-
Summary of Changes2.
-
Clinical Guidelines & Essential Drugs Guideline 3.
-
Biomed Referential4.

 A MSL list can be at End-User Unit (EUU), at Project or at Country level; however

there is a hierarchy among these levels within one country.

o Put in the MSL the non-consumable / equipment which are present at EUU

level but not anymore stored at project pharmacy level.
Examples: delivery & OT tables, laboratory & biomed equipment, etc.

o Check if an article requires other associated or related articles.
Examples: control laboratory tests, oxygen filters, etc.

 Beside the project regular needs, remember to include articles:

o For the Prepositioned stock.
o For Staff Health, PEP kit, first aid kit, etc.

At Coordination level:

A double check should be done with the MSF OCP Medical List. This list includes all
validated articles (available to be used) by MSF OCP5, those articles could be:

 Standard or Non-standard,
 Subjected to medical referent agreement,
 Or subjected to country/project restriction.

If the Medco agrees on the need for articles not in the MSF OCP Medical list or for
restricted articles, a justification needs to be submitted to the Cell Medical responsible.

In the case of articles absent from the MSF OCP Medical List, the “New article request
procedure” should be followed.

At Cell level:

If Cell Medical responsible agrees on the need for articles not in MSF OCP Medical list
or for restricted articles, the approval is needed from the concerning Medical Referent
/ Medical Director and communicated to ParisPharma.

 The MSL should reflect medical protocols in use.
 The MSL should avoid the Non Standard articles if possible.
 For the same drug: avoid multiple strengths / forms as much as possible.
 Oral route should be first option as parenteral / injectable ones are only

recommended in severe cases or when oral route is not suitable (digestive
intolerance, ill-absorption, etc.).
 Preferably restrict use of syrups and suspensions for hospital pediatric wards,

nutritional programs and/or specific programs.
 For a same indication; avoid therapeutic duplicates.

 For more details, refer to Essential Drugs guideline - Part two – Chapters
“Organization and management of a pharmacy” & “Prescription, cost, compliance”.
