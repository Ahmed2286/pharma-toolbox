SOP Point Prevalence Survey (PPS)

INTRODUCTION

Objective of a PPS
A point prevalence antibiotic survey gathers information about antibiotic use at one time point
to provide information that can be used for quality improvement. A PPS is an essential
component of an antibiotic stewardship initiative, especially if combined with measures of
antibiotic consumption.
It comes in complementarity with the rational prescription analysis, which consider randomly
and retrospectively all the molecule prescribed to a patient.

What is Antibiotic stewardship?
Antibiotic stewardship aims to preserve the effectiveness of current antibiotics, so that they
can continue to be used to fight against infectious diseases in the future.
There is a direct link between use of antibiotics and emergence of resistant bacteria.
Antibiotic stewardship thus refers to coordinated interventions designed to improve and
measure the appropriate use of antimicrobial agents by promoting the selection of optimal
antibiotic drug regimens including dosing, duration of therapy and route of administration.

What is a Point Prevalence Survey on Antibiotic use?
The PPS is a one-shot evaluation that takes a picture of all antibiotic prescriptions for all
inpatients in a hospital who are on antibiotics at 8:00 am.
It allows an evaluation of how antibiotics are being used on their project or unit, and allows a
targeted plan to improve patient safety and quality of care by optimizing the use of antibiotics.
A PPS should be done at least once a year (suggested twice) at hospital level or if not possible
in most of the units. It can be repeated more often if needed, in order to follow more regularly
the results obtained by the improvement measures adopted.

This PPS methodology is focused on the quality of antibiotic prescriptions and to allow
complete reflection, must be combined with a tool giving information on the quantity of
antibiotic used (e.g. analysis of Daily Defined Doses (DDD)).

The Antibiotic Resistance Task Force strongly recommends projects to conduct a Point
Prevalence Survey on antibiotic use at least once or twice a year. The recommended tool to
tailor stewardship activities is the MSF PPS Tool.
Projects willing also to participate in the GPPS initiative (cf annex1) should contact the ABR
focal point at headquarters for advice.

This exercise will allow:

• To have a view of the quality of systemic antibiotic prescriptions, oral or parenteral
provided in the hospital/ward one given day (topical, ophthalmic and otic antibiotics are
excluded). According to your capacity and your objectives, you can decide to consider all
antimicrobial prescriptions (systemic antibiotics, systemic antimycotics/antifungals, anti-
tuberculosis drugs, antimalarials, systemic antivirals).
• To identify weaknesses in knowledge or competences and tailor improvements in antibiotic
prescribing.
• To evaluate by service and/or specific criteria chosen.
• To identify the limits and gaps of current reference guidelines.

Be aware, this methodology will not allow:
• To determine if the correct diagnosis has been made.   
• To assess the quality of the clinical examination.
• To assess the quality of the relationship between the clinician and the patient.

CONDUCTING A PPS

Prerequisites:

Leadership
To ensure a consistency of the quality improvement process, a project focal point needs to be
designated; generally this person will be a clinician. The focal point will lead an Antibiotic
Stewardship Committee that ideally includes at least one clinician, one pharmacist, one
member from the IPC team and one microbiologist/laboratory technician if existing. Their
role is to organize and conduct the PPS according to the frequency chosen, animate the
restitution, ensuring choice of actions and their implementation.
The full support of the medical team leader (MTL)/project medical referent (PMR) is key. The
MTL/PMR is responsible for submitting the results to the Medical Coordinator.
Participation of a designated national staff and MoH counterpart from the beginning is
paramount for sustainability.


Local guidelines
To ensure validity of the data collected, the Committee and the Medical Coordinator have to
discuss and state the full reference of the protocols that will be used for the PPS (e.g. MSF
Clinical Guideline 2016, Pediatric Guidelines 2017, etc).

Minister of Health involvement
Introducing PPS to MoH and restituting on results is a practical way in which MSF can advocate
for and contribute to the implementation of Nation Action Plans on ABR.

2-Plan multidisciplinary evaluation teams: The number of evaluators needed depends on the

per team are constituted in advance according to their skills, expertise and availability. Analyze

Collecting the data

1-Ward form: it must be filled early in the morning. It will allow analyzing the prevalence of
patients under antimicrobial drugs. You can take advantage of this time to list the patients
receiving antimicrobial at 8:00 am per ward.

2-Patients included in the survey: all patients who are receiving systemic antibiotics (oral or
parenteral) and who are in the hospital at 8:00 am on the day of survey should be included in
the study (topical, ophthalmic and otic antibiotics are excluded).
Patients hospitalized at 8:00 am with no antibiotics but antibiotic prescription is done during
the ward round after 8:00 are excluded. Patients admitted after 8:00 am are excluded also
even if receiving antibiotics. Patients from day-only or day-stay units are also excluded.

According to your capacity and your objectives, you can decide to consider all antimicrobial
prescriptions (systemic antibiotics, systemic antimycotics/antifungals, anti-tuberculosis drugs,
antimalarials, systemic antivirals) but the workload and the number of patients concerned will
be higher.

3-Note on antibiotic prophylaxis: include any patient who received one or more doses of

Entering of data and analysis

1-Define the prevalence of patients receiving antibiotics (nb of patients hospitalized at 8:00
am/nb of patients receiving antibiotics)

2- Core variables have been identified to allow priority quality improvement initiative (fitting
with the ones from OCG rational prescription tool).
They are either “Yes” or “No” and will give a percentage of agreement.

You can enter these data online into DHIS2 and use the dashboard to look at your results.
If not yet available, you can use the calculation excel sheet “core elements”, to help you with

The tool will calculate the proportions and allow the analysis of the six or seven core variables
on the following components of prescription:
-Diagnosis justifying antibiotic prescription clearly recorded.
-Compliance between valid protocols that have been defined for the project (National and/or
MSF) and the choice of the molecules.
-Agreement between the posology prescribed and diagnosis, age, weight, sex and particular
condition (e.g. pregnancy) according to the protocol. 
-Adequacy of the route of administration. 
-Respect of contraindications.
-If a stop/review date is clearly mentioned  
-Agreement between duration prescribed and diagnosis, age, weight, sex and particular
conditions if appropriate (as complete duration not always assessable the day of the survey)

The rational prescription indicator is automatically calculated based on the results of the seven
core variables.

2-Share the findings and action plan with the MoH when appropriate.

Action Plan on ABR. WHO has produced a PPS methodology that includes extra parameters
such as presence of indwelling catheters, previous history of surgery, underlying medical
conditions and reevaluation of antibiotic appropriateness after several days of treatment.

Mode d’emploi Enquête de Prévalence (EPP)
Novembre 2019

INTRODUCTION

Objectifs d’une enquête de prévalence
Une enquête de prévalence (EPP) est une méthodologie internationale qui analyse, un jour
donné, comment les antibiotiques sont utilisés sur une structure de santé. Une EPP est un
élément essentiel de la dynamique de bon usage des antibiotiques, surtout si elle est
combinée à l’analyse quantitative des consommations d’antibiotiques. Sa réalisation
contribue à l’amélioration de la qualité des soins apportés aux patients.
Elle vient en complément de l’analyse rationnelle des prescriptions qui considère
rétrospectivement l’ensemble des molécules prescrites.

Qu’est-ce que le bon usage des antibiotiques ?
Le bon usage des antibiotiques vise à préserver sur le long terme l’efficacité des antibiotiques.
Il existe un lien direct entre la façon d’utiliser les antibiotiques et l’apparition des résistances
au sein des bactéries.
Le bon usage des antibiotiques est l’ensemble des actions mises en œuvre pour promouvoir
la prescription rationnelle d’antibiotiques aux dose, durée et voie d’administration
recommandées.

Qu’est-ce qu’une enquête de prévalence sur l’utilisation des antibiotiques ?
Une EPP est une évaluation, un jour donné, de toutes les prescriptions d’antibiotiques chez
les patients hospitalisés ce jour-là à 8h le matin.
L’analyse des informations collectées sert aux discussions d’équipe sur les actions retenues
pour améliorer l’utilisation des antibiotiques, la qualité et la sécurité des soins apportées aux
patients.
Cet exercice d’enquête de prévalence doit être effectué au minimum une fois par an (nous
recommandons 2 fois) au niveau de l’hôpital, ou, par défaut, dans la majorité des services de
celui-ci. Il peut être répété plus fréquemment si besoin, afin de suivre de plus près les mesures
correctives apportées.

Une EPP permet une analyse qualitative des prescriptions d’antibiotiques et permet, si
confrontée au suivi quantitatif des consommations d’antibiotiques une réflexion globale sur
l’utilisation des antibiotiques dans une structure de soins.

Cet exercice permet :
• D’avoir une vision globale de la qualité des prescriptions d’antibiotiques systémiques,
oraux ou parentéraux effectuées dans un service/hôpital un jour donné (les antibiotiques
à usage local, ophtalmic, otologiques sont exclus).
Selon vos capacités et vos objectifs, vous pouvez effectuer l’analyse en considérant tous
les antimicrobiens à usage systémique (antibiotiques, antiviraux et antifongiques à usage
systémique, anti-tuberculeux, anti-paludéens). L’exercice sera plus complet mais
également plus lourd à réaliser.
• D’identifier des faiblesses en termes de connaissances ou compétences et de définir un
plan d’amélioration des prescriptions d’antibiotiques pour réduire au maximum
l’émergence des résistances et mieux traiter les malades.
• D’évaluer par service et/ou par critère spécifique choisi.
• D’identifier les limites ou les manquements des référentiels en place.

• De contribuer éventuellement au projet G-PPS. En partageant les données sur la
plateforme en ligne, une analyse descriptive automatique sur les natures des diagnostics,
les molécules utilisées, les types d’indication (communautaire vs associé aux soins) sera
générée. La comparaison avec les données d’autres hôpitaux et d’autres pays permet de
nourrir les réflexions.

Soyez conscients que cette méthodologie ne permet pas :
• De déterminer si le diagnostic a été correctement posé.
• D’évaluer la qualité de l’examen clinique.
• D’évaluer la qualité de la relation entre le clinicien et le/la patient/e.

Les équipes du MdS doivent être informées de la réalisation de ces enquêtes de prévalence.
Introduire cette démarche qualité auprès du ministère, les investir et leur communiquer les
résultats des enquêtes est une façon concrète de contribuer à la mise en place de politiques
de bon usage des antibiotiques/plan d’actions nationaux concernant l’antibiorésistance.
