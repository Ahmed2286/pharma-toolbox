As with the principle also applied to the first line DST on MGIT (SIRE), the test measures the
in-vitro growth of a M. tuberculosis suspension in an enriched liquid medium (MGIT-tube) in
the presence of a selected drug, in comparison with the growth of the same suspension
diluted 1/100 and inoculated in a control drug-free tube. The amount of growth is expressed
in “growth units” (GU), a measure specific for the BACTEC-MGIT960 system and formulated
by Becton Dickinson.
Unlike with SIRE, the instrument software does not include the program for 2nd line DST,
therefore drug results are to be recorded manually in the reports.
For this reason it is very important always to check if the tubes where placed in the MGIT960
machine in the right order (control-O-K-C-E-P) from left to right. This order corresponds to
the order as printed on the AST report from top to bottom.
Usually DST is performed with pure isolates (“indirect method”). But it can also be applied
directly with decontaminated sputum (“direct method”). This is only possible for strongly
positive specimens (at least 2+ on WHO-IUATLD scale for fresh sputum). However this
method is not recommended by this guideline due to the possible presence of
contamination in the sediment, which may lead to false resistant results.

4.2 MGIT procedures for first-line drug susceptibility test

4.2.1 Preparation of the drugs

Each drug tested for FLD is received in lyophilized vials and needs to be reconstituted with
4 ml of sterile distilled water to obtain stock solutions of 40 μg/ml for streptomycin, 5 μg/ml
for isoniazid, 50 μg/ml for rifampin and 175 μg/ml for ethambutol.

The MGIT tube after preparation for DST will contain 7 ml of broth + 0.5 ml of MGIT OADC +
0.5 of inoculum of mycobacteria for a total volume of 8.1 ml, so addition of 100 μL of the
stock solution of antibiotic to the tube, to reach the correct final concentration (table 4.1).

From every reconstituted vial is it possible to prepare aliquots of 100 μL for 40 tests.
Aliquots of 110 μL should be prepared because, when removed from a cryotube with the tip
of automatic pipette, a small amount of antibiotic will be lost.
See “Check list for first-line DST antibiotics preparation” page 52.

Lyophilized antibiotics can be stored at 2-8°C. Once reconstituted, store at -20°C or lower for
6 months, without exceeding the original expiration date. Once thawed, they must be used
immediately.

4.2.4 Inoculation of PZA testing

The BACTEC MGIT960 TB PZA susceptibility test uses a pyrazinamide drug concentration of
100 μg/ml.
The test requires specific MGIT tubes with corrected low pH, and can be inoculated from
either a solid or liquid medium. This guideline describes only the inoculation from liquid
positive culture as recommended method. The first day of MGIT positivity reported by the
instrument is considered Day 0.

For the preparation of the test inoculum, a positive 7 ml MGIT tube should be used the day

A tube which has been positive for longer than five days should be sub-cultured to a fresh

4.3.3 Interpretation and recording of the results

Each drawer on the main screen indicates:
– How many MGIT-broths/ASTs are ongoing.
– How many MGIT-broths/ASTs are positive.
– How many MGIT-broths/ASTs are negative.

For each drawer a report can be printed for all MGIT-broths and MGIT-ASTs present in that
drawer (Inventory report). This report gives an overview of the status of all MGIT-broths and
MGIT-AST’s present in the machine. To print, see “Check list for reporting ongoing and
completed analyses of the BACTEC-MGIT960 machine”.
For more specific reports, see the User’s Manual.

A DST set is incubated until the machine indicates it should be removed. This happens when
the control tube has reached a GU of 400 units, or the control tube has not reached a GU of
400 units but the limit of 14 days has been reached.

A DST result is only considered valid if the control tube shows enough growth (400 growth
units) within the period of 14 days.

A strain is considered resistant to an antibiotic if the growth unit reaches ≥ 100 growing
units (GU) in the corresponding drug-containing tube, or susceptible if the growth unit is
< 100 GU.

The final result with the date of completion and operator ‘s name or initials is registered on
the AS400 working list.

In case of DST failure, the system will not provide an interpretation of the AST set results.
The different error codes printed on the AST report indicate the possible cause:
Set error 400:
The system detects indications of potential contamination, or over-inoculation, or rapid
growing mycobacteria (NTM). The system cannot distinguish between these cases. Over-
inoculation can from either a too-concentrated initial cell suspension, or improper dilution
of the growth control inoculum. Correct actions: inspect inoculum, BA-plate and AST tubes
for contaminants (acid-fast staining to distinguish contamination from NTM). Repeat test in
case of non-acid-fast contaminants.
Set error 200:
The system cannot detect sufficient indication of growth in the growth control tube, and
does not provide an interpretation of the DST set result. The inoculum was too dilute or
nonviable.
Correct actions: review inoculum preparation procedure and repeat test.
Report error in case of DST failure.
See “Check list for removal of completed AST sets from MGIT960” page 60.

4.4 Validation and quality control for 1st and 2nd line DST

4.4.1 Validation of the test

The reference laboratory responsible for supervision of the culture laboratory is in charge of
validation of drug susceptibility testing, according to the laboratory’s performance.

While validation of culture techniques relies mainly on the indicators outcomes, to validate
drug susceptibility testing the laboratory should receive a panel of 30 strains from the
reference laboratory. This panel usually includes a mix of mono-resistant, poly-resistant,
MDR strains, and more than one strain resistant to ethambutol and streptomycin. For
second line drugs are used strains with various pattern of resistance, including XDR strains.
The panel should include approximately 50% of susceptible and resistant strains for each
drug.
The reference laboratory can also request the culture laboratory to send strains with results,
which are then cross-checked by the reference laboratory.
In light of the results for the above procedures, the reference laboratory validates the
technique and discusses any problem encountered during implementation of the test.

4.4.2 Internal Quality Control

As for cultures, internal control includes control of the instrument, of the consumables
every time a new batch is started, and on-going evaluation of the laboratory procedures,
monitoring of good laboratory practice, updating of SOPs, etc.

When a new batch of AST SIRE Kit vials for 1st line is received, quality control is performed
by inoculating the reference strains in drug-containing tubes.
A 1 mg/ml suspension of fully susceptible strain is inoculated in an MGIT tube. When the
culture is positive, it is inoculated in DST tubes following the same procedures used to
perform 1st line DST for clinical samples.
For preparation of the suspension, see “Check list for preparation of 1 mg/ml bacterial
suspension” page 47.. DST is performed according to the procedures described in par 1.2 of
this section. QC results are registered in the QC sheet. The batch of antibiotics can be used
only if the strains show the expected results. In case an incorrect result is obtained, the test
should be repeated. In case of a second incorrect result, the antibiotics should be discarded,
and report the faulty results to the supplier.

For 2nd line DST, each new lot of second line drug stock solutions (OKCEP) should be tested
using a susceptible M. tuberculosis reference strain by culturing the strain in a 7ml MGIT-
broth and subsequent AST (OKCEP). Release of this lot can only be done when a correct
result (sensitive to OKCEP) is obtained. Results of the batch control and release of the batch
are registered in the QC sheet for media preparation. In case that the result is incorrect, the
test should be repeated. In case of a second incorrect result the whole lot should be
discarded and any test performed with this lot should be repeated.

4.4.3 External quality control

After validation of the technique, the performance of FLD and SLD DST during routine
activity is evaluated on a regular base by participating in international rounds for quality
control of AST for M. tuberculosis to first- and second line drugs.
These rounds consist in receiving by the reference laboratory a panel of about 20 strains
each time. To participate contact the National Reference Laboratory of your country.

Check list for disinfection in case of accidental spills

• Put on a new pair of gloves when the upper pair could be infected.

• Cover the infectious material with absorbent paper soaked with 1% chlorine
solution.

• Pour more disinfectant around the paper; this must remain in contact with the
infected material for 20 minutes.

• Remove the chlorine with absorbent paper.

• Deposit everything in the autoclave bag, together with the upper pair of gloves
and autoclave.

• Disinfect again the site of the incident with 1% chlorine solution, then clean with
absorbent paper and sterile water.

• If the liquid has penetrated below the work surface of the biological safety
cabinet, the whole worktop should be taken off and the space below disinfected in
the manner described above.

• Decontaminate the equipment with 70% ethanol.

• In case of a large spill, after covering the spillage with paper soaked with 1%
chlorine, leave the room for 30 minutes (this will allow aerosols to settle).

For spillage out of the BSC

• Warn all the colleagues in the lab.

• Leave the lab as soon as possible.

• Prevent somebody from entering the lab by clearly hanging the ‘STOP’ sign on the
door of the lab.

• Warn the laboratory assistant or responsible.

• Wait for at least 20 minutes before entering the laboratory again to allow aerosols
to settle down or to be removed through the air filter system.

• Put on a mask (present in the air-lock) in the correct way and enter the laboratory.

• Treat the area of the incident as described for an incident inside a safety cabinet.

Check list for sputum decontamination procedure

• Work in BSC wearing gloves, mask and coat.

• Check that all required material is in the BSC: 50 ml Falcon tubes, rack, phosphate
buffer (pH 6.8-7.0), 2% NALC-NaOH-sodium citrate, sterile saline.

• Record on a sterile 50 ml Falcon tube the number of the sample to be decontaminated.

• Pour the amount of 2% NALC-NaOH-sodium citrate necessary to process all samples
in a new sterile Falcon tube to up to 30 ml maximum (if more decontaminant is
needed, use more than one Falcon).

• Place absorbent paper on the working area of the safety cabinet and soak it with
1% chlorine solution. Manipulate contaminated material on top of this paper.

• Pour sample in the tube. Sample should not exceed 10 ml; select most purulent or
mucous portion. If sputum remains attached to surface of container, remove it by
adding few drops of sterile saline.

• Set the timer to 20 minutes.

• Add 2% NALC-NaOH-sodium citrate to sample in 1:1 proportion (considering
sample or sample + sterile saline if latter has been added).

• Close the Falcon tube and vortex for not more than 15 seconds.

• Invert the tube a few times, to be sure that the decontamination solution gets
contact with the entire inner surface of tube, included the cap.

• Leave sample to stand for 20 minutes at room temperature ( 20 to 25°C); during
this period invert the tube several time to facilitate the complete digestion.

• At the end of the 20 minutes, add phosphate buffer (pH 6.8) by pouring from the
bottle, to reach a volume of 40 ml in order to neutralize the NaOH. Keep the falcon
in tilt position to minimize risk of cross-contamination.

• Invert the container several times to mix contents.

• Centrifuge for 20 minutes at 3000 x g.

• Wait for 5 min to allow aerosols to settle.

• Pour supernatant into splash-proof discard container filled with 1% chlorine
solution. If necessary, wipe lip of Falcon tube with chlorine, being very careful to
not allow the disinfectant to flow into tube.

• Sediment samples which have been decontaminated can be stored at 2-8°C for
7 to 10 days.

Check list for LJ medium preparation (for 320)

• Work in ‘media preparation’ room.

• Wear coat, gloves.

• Check that all required material is available: LJ powder; glycerol; eggs; alcohol
95%, 2L Erlenmeyer with stopper; glass beads; glass funnel, 1 L measuring

• Put into the Erlenmayer 600 ml of distilled cold water, previously stored in the
fridge.

• Add 37.2 g of LJ powder and 12 ml of glycerol to the water.

• Mix until it has obtained homogeneous suspension.

• Heat on low flame, shaking continuously. When the suspension starts to boil count
1-2 minutes.

• Stop with cotton and aluminium foil.

• Autoclave at 121°C for 15 minutes.

• Let it cool it down to 50°C.

• Brush 20-25 eggs with soap and clean with alcohol.

• Break the eggs in a 2 L Erlenmeyer containing sterile glass beads. Shake after you
pour each egg, to obtain a well homogeneous mass.

• Filter exactly 1000 ml of egg pouring the egg through four layers of sterile gauze

bunsen burner if necessary before pouring.

• Add 1000 ml of egg to 600 ml of LJ base avoiding formation of air bubbles.

• Mark 320 sterile tubes with date batch number.

• Fill the media dispenser previously sterilized, then distribute 5 ml into each tube,
keeping them in oblique position.

• Place in inspissator at 85°C for 45 min.

• Cool tubes, with lids partially unscrewed, to room temperature in inclined position

• Close the lids.

• Store in the refrigerator at 4°C for maximum 6 months in a box labbeled with
bacth number.

Check list for processing contaminated MGIT cultures

Interpretation of the result

For PNB inoculated from LJ

inoculation of the tube.
