Check list for SLD MGIT from MGIT tube

• Work in BSC wearing gloves, mask and coat.

• Check that all required material is in the BSC: MGIT tubes, rack, OADC, 15 ml
sterile Falcon tube, working drug solutions, 1, 5 and 10 ml gruaduated pipettes,
micropipettes, 10-100 μl tips, physiological saline solution, sterile saline.

• Place absorbent paper on the working area of the safety cabinet and soak it with
1% chlorine suspension. Manipulate contaminated material on top of this paper.

• Place in a suitable rack:
– 6 MGIT-broth tubes (5 for drugs and 1 for GC),
– the MGIT positive culture.

• With a 10 ml pipette fill a 15 ml sterile Falcon tube with 9.9 ml of sterile saline.

• Label all tubes with the ID number then starting from left, label the tubes GC and
then initials O, K, C, E and PAS.

• Add 0.8 m OADC supplement to each to the tubes by using a sterile 5 ml
graduated pipette.

• With a 10-100 μL micropipette, add 100 μL of each drug working solution to the
appropriate tube.

• Discard the leftover working solutions.

• Vortex the MGIT positive culture for few second and allow the aerosols to settle
for 15 min before opening the tube.

• With a 1 ml pipette take 0.5 ml of the MGITpositive culture, and inoculate it in
each of the drug-containing tubes.

• With a 10-100 μL micropipette transfer 0.1 ml of the MGIT positive culture into the
dilution tube containing 9.9 ml of sterile saline in order to prepare a 1:100 dilution.

• With a 1 ml sterile pipette inoculate the control tube with 0.5 ml of this 1/100
dilution.

• Tightly close and mix by gently inverting the tubes.

• And place in MGIT.

Check list for incubation of DST set in MGIT960

• Step 1: Place the tube in a 6-places AST rack.

• Step 2: Open the desired drawer. Select the ‘tube entry’ option.

• Step 3: Scan the barcode of the AST carrier and the additional Epicentre barcode
of the control tube.

• If the option of scanning additional barcodes is not available, select option
‘Accession barcode not available’. The antibiotic settings will not correspond with
your experiment, but to the 1st line drugs, each one repeated.

• Step 4: The dedicated positions are indicated on the screen and the green lights in
the drawer are highlighted. Insert the tubes at the indicated positions. Take care
that the control tube is placed at the left side. In this way the control is placed at
the first position followed by O, K, C, E & PAS.

• Repeat steps 4 & 5 for all new AST carriers you want to insert.

Make sure that all tubes are completely inside the holes before closing the drawer.

Check list for removal of completed AST sets from MGIT960

• Touch any of the membrane buttons on the screen to activate the screen.

• Open the drawer with the tubes to be removed.

• Select the option ‘Remove completed AST sets’. The green lights at the positions
of the first positive AST set will blink.

• Remove the set and scan the barcode of the AST carrier. Place the rack with tubes
temporarily in a tube holder.

• The lights will stop blinking. Repeat these steps for all completed AST sets.

• Close the drawer.

• Print the AST report.

• For each test, strikethrough the standard first-line antibiotics on the printed report
and replace with the antibiotics used in your experiment (OFL,KAN, CAP, ETH, PAS)
The order the tubes were placed in the drawer from left to right corresponds to
the order as printed on the AST report from top to bottom.

Annex 1

Procedures for samples containing Cetylpyridinium chloride-sodium
(CPC)

Cetylpyridinium chloride (CPC), is a quaternary ammonium compound used for partially
digesting and decontaminating sputum in transit20. When a digested/decontaminated
specimen arrives in the laboratory, it is concentrated by centrifugation and the sediment is
directly inoculated onto the medium, preferably after repeated centrifugation to remove
cetylpyridinium chloride. When cetylpyridinium pre-treated specimens are centrifuged, the
cooling system of the centrifuge should be preferably turned off to prevent precipitation of
the decontaminant. For the same reason samples containing CPC should not be stored in
the fridge or in a cool temperature in order to avoid precipitation.

If a laboratory experience high contamination rate for samples treated with CPC, at arrival in
the lab, samples can be additionally treated with NALC-NaOH, with an exposure reduced to
10 minutes compared to the 20 minutes applied to samples CPC-free.

Disadvantage of this method is that the reagent requires minimum 24 hours to digest and
decontaminate the sample, and that CPC is not compatible with MGIT or agar based media,
for this reason, samples treated CPC should be only inoculated on egg agar based media, as
phospholipids neutralize inhibitory action of the reagent14.

Preparation CPC

– Work in a media preparation room.
– Wear gloves, and coat.
– Check that all required material is available: 1% cetylpyridinium chloride (CPC) (for
decontamination), 2% Sodium Chloride (NaCl) (for liquefaction), distilled water, flask.
– Dissolve 10 g of CPC and 20 gr of NaCl in 1000 ml of distilled water (V/ V in H20).
– The solution is self-sterilizing and remains stable for an extended period if protected from
the light, however use of distilled sterile water is recommended.
– Dissolve with gentle heat any crystals that might form in the working solution.

Procedure for samples preparation

– Collect a minimum of 2 ml of sputum in a 50 ml screw cap centrifuge sterile tube (falcon).
– Add an equal volume of CPC-NaCl, cap securely and shake by hand until specimen
liquefies.
– DO not keep the sample with CPC in the fridge but keep it at room temperature.
– Package specimen in a mailing container and send to processing laboratory. The specimen
is digested and decontaminated in transit.
– CPC is added to an equal volume of sputum.

Annex 2

Inoculation of reference strains for MGIT liquid medium QC according
to BD procedures

– Work in BSC wearing gloves, mask and coat.
– Check that all required material is in the BSC: sterile tubes, Middlebrook 7H9, loop, 1 ml
sterile pipette, MGIT960 supplement , MGIT tube.
– Place absorbent paper on the working area of the safety cabinet and soak it with 1%
chlorine solution. Manipulate contaminated material on top of this paper.
– Prepare sterile tubes containing Middlebrook 7H9.
– Take a loop of colonies from the LJ tube. Shake the loop in the plastic tube, letting the
colonies dilute in the broth. The suspension should exceed Mc Farland 1 turbidity.
– Mix on the vortex for 2-3 min to break clusters.
– Allow to stand for 20 min.
– Transfer the supernatant to an empty tube and allow to stand for 15 min.
– With a pipette transfer to a new empty tube and adjust to 0.5 Mc Farland with distilled
water.
– Add 0.1 ml of reconstituted MGIT960 supplement to the MGIT tube.
– With a 1 ml pipette, take 500 μL of the colonies diluted with distilled water and inoculate
in the MGIT tube.
– Incubate at 37°C.

Procedure for inoculation of AST from solid medium according to
BD procedures

– Work in BSC wearing gloves, mask and coat
– Check that all required material is in the BSC: streile tube, Middlebrook 7H9, 1 ml sterile
pipette, loop, glass beads, sterile saline, MGIT tube, SIRE kit, MGIT960 OADC.
– It is important to have fresh growth on a solid medium, such as an LJ slant (within 15 days
of appearance of growth on the medium). Older cultures may result in unreliable
susceptibility test results.
– Add 4 ml of BBL Middlebrook 7H9 broth to a clean sterile tube (approximately 16 x 128
mm in size) with 8-10 glass beads.
– Use growth on solid medium which is no more than 15 days old within 15 days of
appearance of positive growth. Scrape as many colonies as possible with the help of a
sterile loop. Try not to take any medium when removing growth.
– Transfer the growth into the tube with broth and glass beads.
– Tighten the cap and Vortex the tube for 1-2 minutes to break the clumps.
– The turbidity of the suspension should be greater than the McFarland #1.0 standard.
– Let the suspension stand undisturbed for 20 minutes.
– Carefully transfer the supernatant suspension with a pipette into another sterile tube.
Avoid taking any growth which has settled on the bottom.
