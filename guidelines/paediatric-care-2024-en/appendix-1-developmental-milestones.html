Appendix 1. Developmental milestones

2 Months

Cognitive
Communication/Language
Social & Emotional

Holds head up when
on tummy

Moves both arms and
legs

Opens hands briefly

Follows past midline

Watches caregiver as
they move

Laughs and vocalises
(sounds other than crying)

Reacts to loud sounds

Smiles responsively and
spontaneously

Looks at caregiver’s face

Physical & Movement

4 Months

Cognitive
Communication/Language
Social & Emotional

Holds head steady without
support when held.

Brings hands to mouth.

Pushes up on
elbows/forearms when on
tummy

Looks at their hands with
interest.
Makes sounds back when
caregiver talks to them.

Turns head towards the
sound of caregiver’s voice.

Smiles on his own to get
caregiver’s attention.

Looks at caregiver, moves,
or makes sounds to get or
keep their attention.

Physical & Movement

6 Months

Cognitive
Communication/Language
Social & Emotional

Leans on hands to support
themselves when sitting.

Pushes up with straight
arms when on tummy.

Rolls from tummy to back.

Puts things in their mouth
to explore them.

Reaches to grab a toy
they want.

Closes lips to show they
don’t want more food.

Takes turns making
sounds with caregiver .
Knows familiar people.

Laughs.

Physical & Movement

9 Months

Cognitive
Communication/Language
Social & Emotional

Gets to a sitting position by
themselves.

Moves things from one hand
to their other hand.

Sits without support

Bangs two things together.

Looks for objects when
dropped out of sight
(like toy).

Lifts arms to be picked up.
Looks when you call their
name.

Reacts when caregiver
leaves (looks, reaches for
caregiver, cries).

Appendix 1

434

12 Months

Cognitive
Communication/Language
Social & Emotional

Pulls up to stand.

Pincer grasp: picks up
things between thumb and
pointer finger like small bits
of food.

Puts blocks in and out
of cup.

Looks for things they
see you hide, like a toy
under a blanket.

Understands “NO”
(pauses briefly or stops,
when you say it).

Calls a parent “mama” or
“dada” or another special
name.

Plays games with you,
like pat-a-cake.

Physical & Movement

15 Months

Cognitive
Communication/Language
Social & Emotional

Takes a few steps on their
own.

Uses fingers to feed
themselves some food.

Tries to use things the
right way, like a phone
cup or book.

Tries to say one or 2 words
besides “mama” or “dada”, like
“ba” for ball or “da” for dog.

Looks at a familiar object, when
you name it.

Follows directions given with
both a gesture and words
e.g. They give you a toy when
you hold out your hand and say,
“Give me the toy!”.

Copies other children while
playing, like taking toys out of
a container when another
child does.

Shows you an object they like.

Claps when excited.

Hugs stuffed doll or other toy.

Shows affection (hugs,
cuddles or kisses caregiver).

Physical & Movement

18 Months

Cognitive
Communication/Language
Social & Emotional

Walks without holding on to
anyone or anything.

Feeds themselves with
their fingers.

Climbs on and off a couch
or chair without help

Copies you doing
chores, like sweeping
with a broom.

Plays with toys in a
simple way, like
pushing a toy car.

Tries to say three or more words
besides “mama” or “dada”.

Follows one-step directions
without any gestures, like giving
you the toy, when you say,
“Give it to me!”

Moves away from you but looks
to make sure caregiver is close.

Points to show something
interesting.

Helps caregiver to dress
themselves by pushing arm
through sleeve or lifting up foot.

Give It to me

Physical & Movement

24 Months

Cognitive
Communication/Language
Social & Emotional

Walks up steps

Kicks a ball

Plays with more than
one toy at the same
time, like putting toy
food on a toy plate.

Says at least two words together,
like “More milk”.

Points to at least two body parts
when you ask them to show you.

Uses more gestures than just
waving and pointing, like blowing
a kiss or nodding “YES”.

Notices when others are hurt or
upset, like pausing or looking
sad when someone is crying.

Looks at caregivers face to see
how to react in a new situation.

Muaw!

30 Months

Cognitive
Communication/Language
Social & Emotional

Takes some clothes off by
themselves, like loose
pants or an open jacket.

Jumps off the ground with
both feet.

Uses things to pretend,
like feeding a block to a
doll as if it were food.

Follows two-step
instructions like “Put
the toy down and close
the door!”.

Says two or more words, with
one action word, like “Doggie
run!”.

Names things in a book when
you point and ask, “What is
this?”.

Plays next to other children
and sometimes plays with
them.

Duck
Cuac!

Physical & Movement

36 Months

Cognitive
Communication/Language
Social & Emotional

Puts on some clothes by
themselves, like loose
pants or a jacket.

Draws a circle when
you show them how.
Speech all understandable
Interactive play

My name
is Anton

Ruum
Rumm

Physical & Movement

48 Months

Cognitive
Communication/Language
Social & Emotional

Holds pencil or crayon
between fingers and
thumb, not a fist.

Catches a large ball
most of the time.

Draws a person with
three parts

Tells what comes next
in a well-known story.

Names four colours

Talks about at least one thing
that happened during their day,
like “I played soccer!”.

Answers simple questions like,
“What is a coat for?” or “What is
a crayon for?”.

Pretends to be something
else during play (teacher,
superhero, dog).

Asks to go play with children,
if none are around, like ”Can I
play with Alex?”.

Avoids danger, like not jump
from tall heights.

Mama look
at my leaf!

Physical & Movement

60 Months

Physical & Movement
Cognitive
Communication/Language
Social & Emotional

Hops on one foot.

Counts to 10.

Uses words about time, like
“yesterday”, “tomorrow” or
“morning” and “night”.

Pays attention for 5 to 10
minutes during activities
(e.g., during story telling time
or making arts and crafts)

Tells a story they heard or
made up with at least two
events
e.g., a cat was stuck in a
tree and a man saved it.

Follows rules or takes turns
when playing games with
other children.

Sings, dances or acts for you.
