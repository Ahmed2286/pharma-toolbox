10.3 Treatment regimens in operational research conditions

Patients with MDR/RR-TB can be treated under operational research conditions with STRs
(distinct from the STRs described in Section 10.2.1).
Whatever the findings of the operational research, the results should be published as they
may complement findings of clinical trials.

10.3.1 Operational research conditions

The requirements for conducting operational research include:
– A study protocol identifying the inclusion/exclusion criteria, regimen composition,
monitoring schedule including 12-month post-treatment follow-up
– A treatment guide
– A patient informed consent process
– The approval of an ethics committee and the Ministry of Health
– A pharmacovigilance system (active tuberculosis drug-safety monitoring and management,
aDSM)11.

Study protocol templates are available from the Global Drug-resistant TB Initiative (GDI)c and
WHOd.

10.3.2 Treatment regimens

The BPaLC (6Bdq-Pa-Lzd-Cfz)12 regimen is an example of a treatment regimen that can be used
to treat fluoroquinolone-resistant MDR/RR-TB, under operational research conditions only.

10.4 Special situations

10.4.1 Women (pregnant or breastfeeding or of childbearing age)

Pregnant and breastfeeding women should be treated without delay.
STRs are recommended except BPaLM/BPaL regimens due to limited data regarding the safety
of pretomanid in this population.
