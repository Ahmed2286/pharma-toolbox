Appendix 30. Summary of the meningitis vaccination campaign

Excel File 6. “Summary of the meningitis vaccination campaign” is used to compile data
and to calculate automatically the indicators of the vaccination campaign:

– The vaccination coverage
– The ratio of syringes to safety box
– The waste factor for autodisable syringes
– The ratio of reconstitution syringes per vaccine vial
– The rate of utilisation of vaccines.

Use of sheets

Use one file per district. Each file includes:

– 1 “Daily vaccination summary (manual)” sheet
– 10 “Summary vaccination by place” sheets
– 1 “Summary vaccination by district” sheet
– Only fill in the yellow cells
– If required, ask the vaccination advisor to adjust the age groups.

“Daily vaccination summary (manual)”

– This sheet is printed out and filled in each day in the field by the vaccination
supervisor using the vaccination tally sheets of each site.
– The sheet covers a period of 6 days. Supplementary days may be added if
necessary.
– Age groups should be adjusted according to the target age group.
– Catch-up vaccination activities should also be recorded on this sheet.
– Consumption of supplies is recorded at the end of the campaign.

“Summary vaccination by place”

– Fill in this sheet from the manual daily vaccination summary sheets compiled for
each vaccination site.
– Age groups should be adjusted according to the target age group.
– Use one sheet for each town or zone covered. Record the name on the tab.
The file automatically calculates:

– The vaccination coverage by age group
– The number of doses of vaccine used
– The rate of utilisation of vaccines
– The indicators of quality and safety.

“Summary vaccination by district”

This sheet is filled in using the vaccination summary sheets for each place of vaccination.

Appendix 31. Estimating needs: number of meningitis vaccination teams

Excel File 7. “Estimating needs: number of meningitis vaccination teams” is used to
calculate the number of vaccination teams needed according to data. The number of core
teams depends on the size of the population to vaccinate, the expected performance for
each vaccinator and the desired duration of the vaccination campaign.

Use of sheets

– The file includes 5 “Place” sheets numbered 1 to 5. Use one sheet for each area
covered by a health facility. If necessary, copy and paste to add new sheets.
– Only fill in the yellow cells.

“Place” sheet

Record on each sheet

– The region, district and zone
– The target population: age group and percentage
– The name of the zone on the tab
– For each vaccination site: the place name, total population, expected performance
and optimal duration.
The file automatically calculates

– The target population by site
– The number of teams needed per site.

If the number of teams required is higher than the number of staff available, adjust the
duration of the campaign or review the expected performance.

Appendix 32. Roles of members of the vaccination team

Security and information officer

– Inform the population: target age groups, opening hours of the vaccination site and
number of days of vaccination, water points available.
– Organise the flow of arrivals and the queue.
– Check ages (no vaccination below the age of 6 months).
– Move the people to be vaccinated steadily towards the registrars.
– Maintain order.
– Inform the team leader of any problems.

Registrar

– Prepare equipment and supplies: table and chairs, cards, pens, dating stamp and
ink pad.
– Pre-stamp or write the date and other useful information on the vaccination cards
(batch numbers).
– Ask questions to find out the information necessary to fill out the vaccination card:
 Name and forename
 Age in months (if under 1 year) or years
 Address
– Write legibly in pen.
– Give the card to the person to be vaccinated or the accompanying person
explaining the importance of keeping the card safe and producing it at each visit to
the health centre.
– At the end of the day: tidy up.

Vaccinator’s assistant

– At the start of the day, give the vaccine batch number to the registrars.
– Set up in a quiet spot, in the shade, next to the vaccinator
– Organise the work table with the equipment and supplies: table and chair, cool box
with vaccines/solvents, vaccine carrier, injection equipment etc.
– Prepare the safety boxes according to the protocol.
– Check the cold chain regularly: temperature in the cool box and vaccine carrier.
– Reconstitute the vaccine:
 Check the name of the vaccine, the solvent, the expiry date, the appearance
of the vaccine and solvent (colour, clarity), the vaccine vial monitor (VVM)
 Observe strict asepsis
 Dilute one vial of vaccine with one vial of solvent at a time
 Store the reconstituted vaccine in the holes in the foam of the vaccine carrier
 After reconstitution, dispose of the dilution syringe and needle in the safety
box
 Put used vials of vaccines and solvents, separately, in buckets with lids.

Vaccinator

– Set up next to the vaccinator’s assitants, in the shade.
– Set up equipment and check that supplies are complete: vaccine carrier, gloves,
kidney dish, cotton wool, rubbish bags and safety boxes.
– Wear gloves and change them regularly (every 50 vaccinations), wash hands each
time gloves are changed.
– Explain to the patient or accompanying person how to look after the person
vaccinated. Ask for help if required.
– Clean the injection site with cotton wool and clean water.
– Inject the vaccine while observing the proper injection technique and asepsis.
– Dispose of the used syringe directly into the safety box which should be placed
close by, away from the circuit of people. Never recap the needle.
– Direct people vaccinated towards the recorder.
– At the end of the day:
 Close the safety box according to safety rules.
 Count and record on the tally sheet the number of vials of vaccine and
solvents, check the VVM and make a count of remaining supplies.
 Tidy and clean the area and organise waste for transport.

Recorder

– Set up close to and following the vaccinator
– Organise the work table with equipment: table and chair, tally sheets, pens, etc
– Fill out the general information on the tally sheet: team, site, date, batch number etc
– Check the age on the vaccination card
– Make a mark in the appropriate column for the age
– At the end of the day:
 Add up the numbers by age group and the overall total.
 Hand in the tally sheet to the team leader/supervisor
 Tidy up.

Vaccination team leader
In the absence of the team leader, these tasks are divided amongst the members of the
team, with the agreement of the supervisor.
The team leader keeps abreast of the unfolding of the campaign and its practical
organisation.

Each morning:

– Take reception of and check the supplies received (quantities).
– Check that the site is clean and well organised:
 Shady areas for waiting and for the team
 Well-marked circuit and stations correctly organised.
– Ensure each member of the team is present.
– Check the cold chain equipment is out of the sun.
– Ensure there is an area for waste storage, out of reach of the population.

Before the start of the vaccination session:

– Check that the security staff are clear about their roles: messages to give out, target
age group and organisation of the circuit.
– Ensure the registrars have filled out a sufficient number of vaccination cards.
– Check the vaccinator’s assitants have begun to reconstitute vaccines.
– Ensure the recorder has filled out the general information on the tally sheet: team,
site, date, batch number etc.

During the vaccination session

– Check the queue is moving smoothly (no excessive waiting at any station).
– Check that all staff are working well.
– Check that recording on the tally sheets is correct (reliability of vaccination
coverage).
– In the event of problems, immediately inform the supervisor or the logistician as
required.

At the end of the day

– Collect and check the tally sheets and calculate the coverage, vaccine utilisation
rate and the supplies used.
– Check the cold chain.
– Ensure the site is tidy and clean.
– Check that waste is collected according to safety rules and handed over to the
logistician in charge of collection.
– Organise a run-through with the team: the day’s results, strengths and weaknesses,
suggestions for improvement. Thank the team.
– Go through results with the supervisor: analysis of results, overview of the day,
improvements suggested and resources needed.

Quality of activities

1. Cold chain

The full equipment is present (1 cool box + thermometer, 1 vaccine carrier).

The number of ice packs is correct.

The storage temperature is adequate.

No vaccines with activated VVM (if no, state the number).

2. Reconstitution of vaccines

Vaccines and solvents have been checked (name, expiry date, appearance,
VVM).

The solvent used is correct for the vaccine (from the same manufacturer).

Vaccines and solvents are at the same temperature when reconstituted (+2 and
+8 °C).

Reconstitution is performed with ONE syringe and ONE needle per vial.

After reconstitution of each vial, the injection equipment is disposed of in the
safety box.

The vial of reconstituted vaccine is kept in the hole in the foam of the vaccine
carrier.

3. Preparation of syringes.

Rules of asepsis are followed.

Syringes are expelled.

Syringes contain exactly 0.5 ml (depending on the vaccine).

Prepared syringes are given immediately to the vaccinator.

The speed of preparation is appropriate to the flow of people to be vaccinated.

The team is well organised (see diagram), good co-ordination between
preparators and vaccinator.

Unused vials of reconstituted vaccine are disposed of at the end of the day

195

4. Use of safety boxes

All needles/syringes for reconstitution and ADS are disposed of in safety boxes
immediately after use, without recapping.

The volume of syringes in the safety box does not exceed the fill line.

Full boxes are stored in an area away from the reach of the public.

5. Transport of waste

Waste is transported to the site of storage and disposal at the end of the day.

Staff transporting waste to the disposal site wear thick gloves.

Recording and gathering of data

General information (team, place, date etc) is filled in on the tally sheet.

All people vaccinated are correctly noted in the appropriate age group.

Totals and calculations are performed at the end of the day.

Supplies are counted and the condition of the VVM is recorded on the tally
sheet.

Closing the site

Tally sheets are collected and checked (total number of vaccinations, vaccines
used and utilisation rate, condition of VVM, injection supplies).

The site is tidied and cleaned by the team.

Waste is prepared for transport and stored in a protected area.

A rapid run-through of the day is performed with the team on site (sharing of
results, difficulties encountered, points for improvement, solutions to share,
thanks for the work).

– Blank sheets may be copied and pasted as required

– The name of the region and district
– The age group of the target population
– The percentage of the total population comprised by the target population
– The volume of a dose of vaccine in cm3

– The number of doses per vial
– The name of each site of vaccination, the total population and the population already
vaccinated in previous days.

– The population as yet unvaccinated
