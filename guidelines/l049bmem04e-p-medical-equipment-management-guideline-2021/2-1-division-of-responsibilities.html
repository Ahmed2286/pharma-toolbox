All aspects of medical equipment require close collaboration between medical, logistics and
supply staff. It is essential that everyone clearly understands their role.

DIVISION OF RESPONSIBILITIES

Management of medical equipment within an MSF mission is a shared responsibility
between the medical, logistics and supply teams. The medical team has the role of
prescriber and user. The logistics team has the role of installer and maintainer. Both are
supported by the supply team for procurement and after-sale service.

The medical team will define the (medical) needs of any (new) medical devices that are
aimed at improving or expanding medical activities. Usually this will be done in consultation
with the MedCo and/or HQ. After the equipment is put into service the medical staff on the
project will take responsibility for safe use, care, regular cleaning and user maintenance and
reporting any technical problems to the logistician in charge and reporting any device
deficiency with potential impact on the end-user and/or patient safety to the pharmacist.
The medical team is also responsible for the consumption, monitoring and ordering of
consumables.

The logistics team will assess the technical needs before an order is made (infrastructural
needs, power, buildings, etc.) and prepare any changes needed. Also, this is usually done in
consultation with the LogCo and/or HQ. Ordering and follow-up are done through the supply
team. On delivery the logistics team will ensure correct installation, and afterwards the
logisticians in charge will ensure that regular technical maintenance is done and will organise
repair in case of breakdown. The logistics team is responsible for ordering spare parts used
during technical maintenance and repair work. Information on acquiring the required skills
is in chapter §3: Recruiting & Training.

At MSF International Office level, the responsibilities are also shared. New equipment is
requested by the medical Working Groups in line with evolutions in treatment
recommendations or diagnostic needs. Equipment which is available on the market is
evaluated from the perspectives of clinical relevance, suitability, ease of use, compliance to
international regulations and quality standards, compatibility of accessories and
consumables, compatibilities with our infection control procedures and equipment,
robustness and the technical support available in the missions. This is done under the
auspices of the Medical Directors, the International Medical Devices coordinator and the
Medical SPINCO (Coordination & Source of Product Information), formerly called ITC
(International Technical Coordination), so that equipment which is standard for all sections is
included in the medical catalogues. Technical support is given by the Biomedical Working
Group. The International Medical Devices Coordinator is also in charge of the coordination
of quality complaints (device deficiencies) and batch recall management when more than
one OC is affected. This follows the Medical Quality Complaint SOP.
