LOCAL SUPPLIERS & CONTRACTORS

If you intend to order equipment locally, it is important to be sure that the supplier will offer
a good level of technical support for training and servicing and a reliable supply of the
consumables and spares needed. Equally, local contractors for servicing and repair work
must be technically competent and reliable and offer guarantees.

For mission countries using a validation database, refer to that first for approved
distributors. For mission countries under regional intersectional pharmacist support, contact
them for supplier assessments and validation support. For other mission countries contact
your HQ Pharma for guidance and validation.

Some considerations when assessing the options for local suppliers and contractors:

•
Official distributors or servicing agents of a recognised manufacturer are preferred as
they are more likely to have the specific training needed and are usually regularly
quality audited by the manufacturer. They may offer the same guarantee (warranty)
levels as offered by the manufacturer, and some distributors may be able to offer
remote technical support even when visits to the project may be difficult.
•
Business links with established European companies can give greater assurance that
the company has good financial backing and will be in business for longer.
•
Check on staff qualifications and training. Official manufacturer training on specific
devices must have been followed.

A survey of the local or regional market can be done by the mission supply/log team in
support of the mission Pharmacist. This can be done using the “Biomedical - Equipment
Supplier Questionnaire and Guideline” with the support of the Technical Referent. This
evaluation requirement includes official distributors, as even this status does not guarantee
equivalent levels of service support as the equipment manufacturer. Some are about as
useful as an ashtray on a motorbike.

As previously mentioned, local-purchase validation requests should be compiled by the
mission pharmacist, and validation is coordinated by the headquarters Pharma team.

STRUCTURES

In MSF projects you find anything from simple tents or semi-permanent structures to
existing buildings which are modified for MSF's activities or new structures built by MSF.

Dust, heat and humidity are big "biomed killers". The equipment often contains electronic
circuits, precision parts, seals and gaskets which are sensitive to these elements. In addition,
some items of medical equipment are themselves a hazard (e.g. radiation from radiology
equipment), so the surroundings need to be protected.

Consider the following questions:

•
Is heat, humidity or temperature stability an issue (laboratory equipment or other
equipment with limits on operating temperatures, etc.)? Temperature and humidity
requirements for the operation and storage of equipment are always mentioned in
the manufacturer’s documentation.
