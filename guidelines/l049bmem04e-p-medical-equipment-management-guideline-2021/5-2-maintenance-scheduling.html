Local supplier (must be validated and guarantee availability).

MSF Supply Centre.

Make sure there is a contingency plan for each project and the whole mission.

The criticality assessment is not the same as the device safety classification (Class IIa, IIb,
etc.). The classification of medical devices is a 'risk-based' system based on the vulnerability
of the human body’s taking into account the potential risks associated with the device. The
criticality assessment is aimed at defining how essential any device is for our medical
activities.

MAINTENANCE & SCHEDULING

An ounce of prevention is worth a pound of cure. The benefits of conducting regular
preventive maintenance on the equipment are: increased life expectancy, enhanced ability
to assess condition, improved safety and availability. Be sure you have comprehensive
maintenance schedules for all medical equipment. In each of the medical services there
should be planning and follow-up of the user maintenance tasks for the equipment in that
service. The Biomedical Service must make a technical preventive maintenance schedule for
all equipment in the project in each service and in the stocks based on the inventory (see
5.4).

Lack of regular preventive maintenance is the biggest cause of breakdown

for medical equipment, and a big factor in defects being found at the

moment the equipment is needed.
