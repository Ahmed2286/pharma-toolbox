POWER SUPPLY

Poor quality power systems can endanger patients and staff and damage equipment. The
power sources for your equipment need to be suitably stable, and the electrical installation
must provide good levels of protection.

Power sources for electrical energy are usually grid power or generator(s) and the quality
and reliability can vary dramatically. Generators are usually more reliable and provide a
more stable electrical supply than grid power; however, they are more expensive to run and
require regular maintenance. The best solution for your situation will depend on the options
at your disposal.

Keep the following in mind:

•
Continuity of service (power outages, generator running hours, etc.).
•
Stability (voltage variation, surges etc.)
•
Operating voltage (most countries have ~230V/50Hz, some are the US ~120V/60Hz
system)
•
Power demands of the equipment. Sometimes individual equipment is very power
hungry (e.g. autoclaves); other times the electricity demand is high because there are
many similar devices (e.g. oxygen concentrators).
•
Backup power options (generator, UPS, etc.).

A proper evaluation of the available power sources is essential in order to

determine the most suitable power supply strategy.

Electrical systems must be of a high standard and offer good protection because medical
equipment is often either sensitive to voltage variations or in some cases needs an
uninterrupted supply.

Medical equipment needs power protection. Some medical equipment has batteries
integrated so continuity of power supply is not jeopardized, other equipment must have
additional uninterrupted power supply. Choosing the proper protection will depend on the
equipment and so it is important to understand the basic functioning of protection device
and systems.

The basic types of electrical protection are:

•
Electrical Safety (infrastructure and personnel)

o Earthing (Grounding) connects the equipment to the earth via a protective

earth (PE) conductor (wire). If the equipment develops an internal fault that
risks putting externally accessible surfaces becoming live, the PE conductor
provides a path for electrical current to flow away from the users and should
trigger the protective devices below. Depending on the earthing arrangement
this would trip the RCD or even the circuit breaker. All outlets should have an
earth pin and PE conductor to protect the user and equipment.
o Circuit breakers protect against fire caused by short-circuits and/or overload.

All circuits must have a circuit breaker sized according to its characteristics.
o Residual Current Devices (RCD's) are for personnel safety and trip or cut power

if there is a ground fault on an equipment. All end user circuits must have a 30
mA RCD protection.

•
Equipment Protection

o Voltage stabilisers will provide the rated output voltage (+/-, depending on its

characteristics) if the supply voltage is in its working range. It does not provide
continuity of service nor have batteries.
o Voltage limiters limit the voltage supplied to the medical equipment. If the

mains voltage is out of range, it will cut the supply. If the voltage is in the
range, it will allow power to go through. It does not provide a stable voltage,
nor does it provide continuity of electrical service.
o Surge protection will protect from very short, but powerful, surges coming

from the network due to manipulations or to lightning. Efficient surge
protection is often best installed in the distribution board but may also be
provided by voltage limiters or integrated in some voltage stabilisers.
o Charger inverter is a pass-through type technology, sometimes called an

offline UPS, which directly supplies AC power to equipment when it is
available. It does not stabilise the supply if the voltage is within its working
range. Once the supplied voltage is out of the working range, or if there is a
power outage, it will switch to battery mode to supply the medical equipment
with power. This equipment is usually coupled with stabilisers to have an all-
around solution - stabilisation and continuity of service.
o Online UPS is equipment where the supplied electricity is always channelled to

charge the battery (bank). Power output is taken from the batteries and
converted to AC voltage. An online UPS always stabilises the power, not only
in case of power outage. Both Online UPS and Charger Inverter equipment
needs to have battery banks sized appropriately in order to have the desired
autonomy.

Any equipment protection must be sized according to the power demand.
