TRAINING OPPORTUNITIES

Skills exchange

Exchange of staff between projects can be a good way for them to learn new skills. If
another project, another MSF section or even another organisation has skilled and
experienced staff, try to arrange a work exchange. A trainee period on another project will
not only provide new practical skills but also can often give new perspectives and insights.
This is equally true for clinical and biomedical staff.

With the adoption of various communication tools in MSF there are also possibilities to
exchange with colleagues in other projects and other missions through organised discussion
platforms. Anyone with an MSF email address can use this platform and create both closed
and open membership focus groups.

Local training

Local training sessions can be organised by you in the project and should be an on-going
activity. Short sessions to deal with specific topics can be quickly organised for selected staff,
based on needs identified during supervisor team meetings. They are instructional but also
give the opportunity to have direct and open discussions with the staff involved. Regular
refresher training sessions should also be organised. This provides an opportunity for staff to
demonstrate the use, cleaning and basic maintenance that can be done at user level and can
be part of annual education and continuing competencies evaluation.

Any clinical staff training sessions, organised by the clinical supervisor, which are related
directly to medical equipment, should involve the IPC Focal Point and the Biomedical
Technician to ensure that discussions cover all perspectives. Combined training sessions also
serve to reinforce team collaboration. The clinical supervisor can focus on correct choice and
use of equipment in line with clinical guidance. The IPC Focal Point can ensure that staff
understand equipment-specific cleaning and disinfection procedures, and the biomedical
person in charge can explain the technical possibilities and limitations of the equipment.

In hospitals in higher income countries it is common to have “Technical Nurse” positions in
each medical service, who ensure training and coaching for the nursing staff on the use and
care of equipment. In MSF we sometimes assign a member of the project’s clinical team as
Nurse Focal Point for the Biomedical Service who plays a similar role.

Some medical equipment requires technicians from an official distributor, trained by the
manufacturer to do the installation and commissioning. Often those technicians will provide
basic on-site training for the project staff. Some official distributors may also provide
training at their local offices for other equipment supplied by them.

Formal training from other organisations

Formal training can be arranged locally in some countries. For clinical staff there may be
training colleges or university hospitals at which MSF can arrange short courses, and there
may also be schools or colleges offering courses in electrical- or information-technology
subjects for the logistics staff.

It may even be possible to pay staff to take part time degree qualifications. For biomedical
positions it can also be arranged for them to follow equipment-specific training sessions to
qualify them to do maintenance work on more complex devices. Many manufacturers

organise multiple training opportunities each year that are often centralised at the
company’s headquarters. In some cases, online training courses are also offered.

This is a longer-term approach as these courses will either require an extended absence
from the project or a long part-time commitment.

Further MSF training support

MSF offers a wide range of different options for training support.

Training or coaching can be offered by Medical Advisors or Technical Referents from each
section, or in some cases the Learning & Development department of an OC will also have
teams of trainers for specific topics. All sections have either regional or mobile (“flying”)
support people who can visit missions to assist with training or individual coaching. This is
frequently the case for IPC, biomedical and for both general clinical topics and specific
services (e.g. paediatrics, IPC, etc.). Your operational manager will be able to advise you on
the options available.

The MSF Academy develops several training curricula in hospital nursing, anaesthetic
nursing, infectious diseases, OPD and strategic medical management. These are sometimes
developed in collaboration with universities and range from on-the-job training to
scholarship programmes. Information can be found on msf.org/academy.

A wide variety of training courses for medical and logistical staff are organised in the MSF
training centres and headquarters. These have the advantage of bringing participants
together from different missions and sections and provides valuable experience-sharing as
well as providing a learning environment away from the distractions of project activity.

The selection of distance learning training courses is also developing rapidly as the
technology to facilitate this becomes more widely available. Both autonomous learning
modules and blended learning courses are available covering general introductions to MSF,
cross-cutting topics that concern all staff and specialised training courses for specific target
groups. These training courses have the advantage of the staff only needing to devote a few
hours at a time and so can be arranged around regular activities. More information can be
found on tembo.msf.org.

Details of all available training courses are available through the Learning &
Development/Training Unit of each section and in the MSF Training Catalogue.
