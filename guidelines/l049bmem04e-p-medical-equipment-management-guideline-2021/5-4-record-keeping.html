RECORD KEEPING

Keeping records of your equipment and all maintenance work will provide a complete
history of each device in the same way that a vehicle logbook records the service history of a
Land Cruiser. This can be very useful in helping trace defects and assessing the general
condition of a machine to decide if it needs replacing.

The medical equipment inventory is the primary tool for record-keeping. Ensure that all
medical equipment is included, both equipment in use and equipment in stock. If a project
has only a very small quantity of medical equipment, this can be updated in the overall
inventory system used by the logistics team. In all other cases, and as the Biomedical
Technician is the overall supervisor for the medical equipment inventory, it is better to keep
the biomedical inventory separate from the main inventory. Various biomedical inventory
tools exist, from simple Excel files to Computerised Maintenance Management Systems. Use
the system that your section has chosen as the standard tool. Make sure that the equipment
inventory is coordinated with the pharmacist to ensure that equipment in the medical stock
is included

If you use a “paper-based” system for maintenance record-keeping, the archive 'hardware'
you use is less important than the way you arrange the information. If you have little
medical equipment then a few files (binders) will do; if you have a lot, use a filing cabinet.
What is important is that the information be logically organised and can be accessed quickly
by the Biomedical Technician. The best place to store these records is therefore in the
biomedical workshop. In some cases (e.g. QC testing of lab equipment) it may be useful to
keep parallel copies for reference in the medical service.
