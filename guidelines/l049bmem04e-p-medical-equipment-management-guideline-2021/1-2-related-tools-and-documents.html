INTRODUCTION

P a g e |3

Each project must have:

•
Medical staff who are all qualified and trained to use and care for the equipment
they use – to ensure safe diagnosis/treatment and to extend the equipment lifespan.
•
A Biomedical Service, usually managed by the Logistics team, which is trained and
qualified to maintain the equipment and which is given the responsibility of
managing and conducting the technical maintenance of the medical equipment - to
ensure the correct functioning and safety of the equipment.
•
Workshop facilities that are well located and suitably equipped.
•
An up-to-date inventory and comprehensive planning for regular preventive
maintenance and record keeping for each machine.
•
Good consumption monitoring and the regular ordering of spare parts and
consumables
•
A plan for backup equipment and spare parts stock in case of breakdown.
•
A strategy for donations (accepting and giving) and disposal.

RELATED TOOLS AND DOCUMENTS

As this guideline is for all sections of MSF, there is no mention of section-specific tools and
documents. Rather, references to tools which can be useful in managing medical equipment
are talked of in terms of their functionality. Although a good deal of effort is being made to
harmonise tools between sections there can still be advantages to different sections trying
different tools.

All sections have platforms for sharing tools and documents. Refer to those to see which is
currently the standard for your section.

CONTACT

If, after discussions in your project or coordination team, you have any questions or
suggestions relating to this guideline, or questions on medical equipment that are not
answered in the guideline please contact your HQ for advice.

Contact for matters of:

•
Equipment choice: your OC medical and Nursing-Care advisors and Pharma/Medical
Device support team.
•
Equipment use: your OC medical and nursing-care advisors.
•
Cleaning and disinfection: your OC IPC advisors.
•
Equipment QA (Quality Assurance, i.e. device deficiency): your OC Pharma/MD
Advisor and Biomedical Referent.
•
For technical matters relating to equipment selection, maintenance and repair: the
Biomedical Advisors.

Given that support for medical equipment requires a very broad set of skills and knowledge,
the advisor you contact may bring in other advisors to supplement the discussion and ensure
the most complete advice possible.
