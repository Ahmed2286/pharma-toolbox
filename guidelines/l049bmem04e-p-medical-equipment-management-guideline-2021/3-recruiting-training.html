3 RECRUITING & TRAINING

This chapter covers the following topics:

•
The types of skills and qualifications needed for the various positions involved in
managing medical equipment.
•
The recruitment process.
•
Coaching and training.

Medical equipment directly affects staff and patient health, so all staff must have the skills
to use, care for and maintain the equipment well. It is essential to recruit qualified staff and
regularly review their training needs.

QUALIFICATIONS & SKILLS

Medical Services

When recruiting clinical staff, clearly clinical qualifications and experience will be the main
focus. Each country has their own approach to training and different certification criteria,
however in many cases this follows a similar pattern. A period of college/university
education, often in collaboration with a university hospital in the capital or other major
towns, is followed by placement in a tertiary facility.

In some countries the more peripheral health facilities will be much less well equipped and
sometimes with less senior or experienced supervisors to oversee professional
development. This can lead to the adoption of practices which are not to the standards that
MSF strives to implement, both in the clinical sense and in terms of the understanding of
medical equipment best practices.

There can also be differences in course curricula between countries, so not all subjects may
be given equal attention, for example in much of the developed world IPC is integrated into
all clinical courses, but globally this is not always the case. Consequently, it can be useful to
know the course curricula to be better able to plan for further training on these aspects for
all clinical staff.

Biomedical Service

Biomedical Technician and Biomedical Engineer are recognised studies in many countries.
They combine technical and medical subjects, thereby also giving graduates a knowledge of
physiology and medical vocabulary, which can facilitate interactions with the medical team.
Qualifications are possible from certificate/diploma up to masters-degree level. All will cover
basic mechanical, electrical and electronic engineering, possibly combined with computer
sciences and robotics and can be followed by specialisations in a wide range of aspects
relating to bioinstrumentation, biomaterials, biomechanics, clinical engineering, medical
imaging and orthopaedic bioengineering. The term “biomedical” can be used to refer to a
wide range of positions and qualifications; sometimes the term Clinical Engineer is also used.
Not all are suitable for biomedical positions in MSF. Here, also knowing the training course
curricula is useful, and more generalist qualifications with experience in a health care facility
are the most pertinent.
