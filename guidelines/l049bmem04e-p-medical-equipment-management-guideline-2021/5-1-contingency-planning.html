With careful structuring and planning, you can avoid many common problems. Preparing
contingency plans in case of failures will reduce the consequences of a failure; good
maintenance will reduce the chance of failure.

CONTINGENCY PLANNING

At some point, the equipment will have problems, even if you think you have done
everything you can to ensure that equipment is well cared for and maintained regularly. Be
prepared for this so that when it happens the impact on the related medical activities will be
limited. This means having equipment or spare parts in backup or having a local service
support contract.

The best way to evaluate which equipment is most likely to breakdown is to keep complete
and accurate maintenance records and a clear follow-up and reporting of breakdowns and
repairs by the Biomedical Service. These will help highlight to the medical teams which
equipment poses the biggest risks for interrupting service.

A complementary method is the criticality assessment. This is a method whereby you
evaluate each type of equipment against a number of criteria which influence the chance of
a breakdown, the consequences of that breakdown and the possible speed of repair. A
simple calculation will highlight which items of equipment are the most critical in the
medical services, so help you define a backup strategy.

As the main impact of any breakdown will be on the clinical activities, the clinical team has
the principle responsibility of ensuring the contingency plan for continuity of service in case
of equipment breakdown. The risk of failure and the time for repair will be evaluated based
on the experiences of the Biomedical Service. The assessment should be done by the MedCo
with the support from the LogCo, together with the project staff.

This method is commonly used in health facilities and a simplified Criticality Assessment Tool
has been created to help with this on an MSF project. Instructions are included with the
tool, and it is available on each section’s platform for sharing tools and documents.
