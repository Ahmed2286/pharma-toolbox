8 DONATION & DISPOSAL

This chapter covers the following topics:

•
Receiving donations
•
Making donations
•
Disposal of equipment

Accepting or making donations can cause many difficulties in the long run. There can be
good reasons, but consider the consequences first, and ensure that donations are in line
with the MSF Donation Policy for Medical Products and Health Technologies. If a donation is
not possible, dispose of unwanted equipment correctly.

RECEIVING DONATIONS

Other MSF sections or organisations may offer medical equipment. This often happens when
projects close, and it can be tempting to accept these donations. If you decide to accept the
donation, it will become a part of the inventory, either for use or for backup, and it must be
maintained to ensure continued service. If for any reason the equipment is difficult to
maintain, accepting it will ultimately only lead to frustration for everyone.

Donation reception qualifying questions.

•
Is there a medical need for the equipment, and is it in line with operational strategy?
The Medical Standard List for the mission/project is a useful reference for this.
•
Does the equipment include all accessories needed to use it in accordance with MSF
medical protocols?
•
Is it possible to find spares and consumables?
•
Is there support documentation available (user and/or service manuals)?
•
Is the history of the equipment known: supplier contact, purchase conditions and
guarantee, maintenance records?

If the equipment is suitable, follow the procedure for pharma validation of local purchases
before accepting the donation.

If the equipment is not appropriate but there is a medical need for similar equipment, you
should consider choosing new equipment. See chapter §4.2: Selection of Equipment.

MAKING DONATIONS

In many health facilities in contexts where MSF works you will find biomedical equipment
lying unused, simply because there are no parts; the skills are not there to maintain and
repair them; or there is simply no money to keep them running.

Some of our missions are subject to (strict) export regulations where we need to indicate the
MSF end-user when purchasing equipment from certain suppliers. Not all suppliers will allow
us to donate to another non-MSF party. When donations are considered, this should be
checked with the medical-items compliance team of the MSF supply centre from which it
was ordered.
