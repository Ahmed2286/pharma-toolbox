ORDER CALCULATIONS AND ORDERING

Aside from equipment orders you will have to make regular orders for consumable and
spare parts, and sometimes accessories will also need to be replaced. There are a number of
sources of information to help you define which parts you need for each item of equipment
and in some cases the tools available to help decide how many. These sources also include
MSF codes and often manufacturers’ reference numbers which you must use when ordering
to be sure that you receive the part you need.

All items used by MSF and purchases are compiled in UniData: STD for items that are in the
SPINCO catalogues and validated by all sections, and NST for items that are not common to
all sections but are requested by a specific section for a specific purpose. For medical
equipment and for the majority of related items, the database includes photographs to aid
identification. Accessories, consumables and spare parts that are from the equipment
manufacturer will also include the manufacturer’s code in the description.

Various tools exist to access the information in the database, which can provide lists of
related articles for each item of equipment. This includes spare parts as well as accessories
and consumables. If special test equipment is needed for the Biomedical Service to conduct
functional tests (preventive maintenance), these are also mentioned.

Manufacturers’ documentation often provides lists of parts with a manufacturer's part
number for non-standard equipment or spare parts which have not yet been assigned an
MSF order code. If any items are needed which are not yet coded then contact your
pharmacy team to request the coding of items needed for use or the Biomedical Referent
for spare parts.

The (User and Technical) Maintenance protocols give detailed information on the individual
items which need to be changed during regular use or as part of regular preventive
maintenance. Define consumables and spare-parts (medical and logistics) needs for each
item of equipment using the documentation listed above.

Define needs using the expected consumption based on use and maintenance requirements,
and verify this against past consumption figures. If no consumption figures are available, the
pharmacy or the supply team can probably help you with past consumption quantities from
the pharma stock records or the order database. For new activities the medical advisors may

For example, you have 10 oxygen concentrators. Each is serviced every 3

months and requires a new air filter at this time. Every year the
antibacterial filter must be changed; therefore the order for 1 year will be

10 x 4 x 1 = 10 air filters and 10 x 1 x 1 = 10 antibacterial filters.

Define spare parts for repair based on your own assessment of the risks (from the
contingency plan) and the recommendations of the Biomedical Referent. Order what you
think you will need, but never order spares that you do not have the capacity (training or
tools) to install correctly.

It is vital that all repairs be done well. Having spares that the Biomedical Technician is not
trained to install can lead to failed attempts at repair. At best this will simply waste money,
at worst this could endanger the life of patients or staff.

In case you have any doubt about which parts to order, contact the Biomedical Referent.

The medical team is responsible defining the needs for equipment, accessories, medical
consumables and parts for user maintenance.

The logistics team is responsible for ensuring good technical support by defining quantities
of parts for preventive maintenance and critical spare parts for repair work.

Ordering will be done through the current supply software. It can be useful to collate the
needs of the different medical services and the Biomedical Service into one single order, and
prepare the order using the current tools used by the pharma for medical ordering before
transferring it into the supply system. Coordinate this with your supply team.

To be sure you receive the items you need, be sure to include the MSF code and description
for each order line, and check with the manufacturer documentation that this is the correct
part. If there is no MSF code for the item you wish to order, be sure to at least include the
manufacturer's name, the model of the equipment and the manufacturer’s part number.

Be sure that each order line includes a full description and a code. Non-

coded items must include at least a manufacturer’s reference code.

Reception of the orders containing any medical equipment or related items should be done
together by the project’s pharmacist (medical stock supervisor) and the biomed. This will
help in identification (i.e. MSF codes cross-checked with manufacturer references) and
storage, as well as ensuring the correct articles and quantities are received in proper
condition. Missing items can be clearly communicated to the supply team.
