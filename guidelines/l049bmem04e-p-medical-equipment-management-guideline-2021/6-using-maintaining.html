6 USING & MAINTAINING

This chapter covers the following topics:

•
Medical staff activities .
•
Biomedical Service (logistics) activities .

Good care and regular preventive maintenance are vital in order to extend the life of your
equipment. Regular maintenance can also help identify potential problems before they lead
to equipment failure.

CLINICAL STAFF ACTIVITIES

As previously described in the Roles & Responsibilities chapter the clinical staff are
responsible for care of the equipment and ensuring cleaning and disinfection, and small
preventive maintenance tasks are carried out.

The tasks and frequencies for each item of equipment are listed in the "User Maintenance
Protocol (I) for many items of MSF standard equipment and in the equipment
documentation.

Medical staff (clinical and hygiene) must be instructed on how to perform these tasks well
for the items of equipment they use or care for. Not all staff use equipment regularly, and
staff turn-over also means it is a good idea to organise regular training sessions. The medical
supervisor should draw-up a schedule for training sessions to cover nursing care and IPC
topics with technical support by the Biomedical Technician.

Remember: The Biomedical Technician can only give technical advice relating to use, care
and the cleaning of the equipment. It is the responsibility of the clinical team to ensure that
all IPC procedures are followed and that the staff knows how to use the equipment correctly
for the treatment or diagnosis of the patient(s)!

Follow-up sheets must be used for each item of equipment to ensure that the tasks are
performed. Model follow-up sheets can be found on the sharing platform for tools and
support documents of each operational section. The sheet header should be filled-in, stating
the type of equipment and the serial number or MSF code and giving details of the activities
to be performed (i.e. cleaning and any checks which need to be done to verify the
equipment is working well). For all devices, follow-up sheets should be kept in a binder per
service to avoid IPC risks of material being attached to the equipment itself.

Clinical staff supervisors must ensure that the tasks are done and the follow-up sheets filled-
in. The Biomedical Technician should also check this periodically and discuss with the
medical supervisor if support or further training is needed.

A copy of the user maintenance protocol and user manual should ideally be kept in each
room of the health facility for quick reference.

All medical equipment must be cleaned and disinfected before it is given to the Biomedical
Service for any maintenance work.
