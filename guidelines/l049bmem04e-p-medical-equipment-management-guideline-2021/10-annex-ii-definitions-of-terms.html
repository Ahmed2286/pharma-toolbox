10 ANNEX II: Definitions of Terms

Medical equipment

Medical devices requiring calibration, maintenance, repair, user training and
decommissioning.

Backup equipment

Equipment which is kept in stock to replace medical equipment in the clinical area in case of
breakdown, thereby ensuring continuity of service. This equipment is normally defined in
the contingency plan, following a criticality assessment of the medical equipment used in a
health facility. Managed by the medical team, this will normally be in the main medical
stock, but for certain equipment it may also be at a central point in the health facility.

Replacement equipment

Equipment which is used to temporarily replace medical equipment which has been
removed for preventive maintenance by the Biomedical Service. Normally this equipment
will be managed by the Biomedical Service and will only be required when an item of
equipment is so intensively used that it cannot be out of service even for the time to do the
maintenance.

Accessories

Supplementary reusable parts or devices, used in combination with an item of medical
equipment, to provide specific or additional functionality (e.g. ultrasound probes, barcode
scanners, transport bags, warming sleeves).

Consumables

Items requiring periodic replacement. Replacement can be required in order to reduce
cross-infection risks (e.g. cannula, masks, anti-bacterial filters, syringes, tubing, etc.) or to
ensure continued performance of the device (e.g. air [dust] filters, gaskets, etc.)

Spare parts

Integral components which are replaced in order to restore the functioning, safety or
physical integrity of the equipment. Spare parts can either be purchased new or, when
within manufacturers’ tolerances and local legislation, be removed and retained for future
use during the decommissioning of equipment.

Preventive maintenance

Work which is carried out regularly to ensure the continued functioning of equipment,
extend lifespan and reliability and ensure safety. This includes inspection before use,
cleaning and additional periodic functional and safety checks. This involves the exchange of
consumables and accessories and/or replacement of spare parts.

Corrective maintenance

Work which is carried out on an item of equipment in order to restore the functioning,
physical integrity, safety and/or performance of a device after failure. This can be to repair
an item of equipment after a breakdown or as part of preventive (technical) maintenance if
spare parts are discovered which are outside the manufacturer’s functional tolerance.

Quality Control (QC)

A test procedure used to verify that a diagnostic device is functioning within specified
tolerances. Often this involves introducing a product or signal with a known value and
comparing it with the value measured by the device. Examples are control solutions for
haemoglobin and glucose point-of-care devices or the use of patient simulators to verify ECG
equipment or multi-parameter monitors.

Calibration

The process of comparing measurements made by a device against a known standard value
in order to determine the accuracy of readings and making adjustments or repairs to correct
errors. As this is rarely possible without specific test equipment or training, this is normally
conducted only by the manufacturer.

Decommissioning

Taking equipment out of active service. This can be done in order to re-commission (install)
it in another facility or to prepare it for donation. More often this is done because
equipment is at end-of-life, so no longer reliable, economically repairable or is no longer
supported by the manufacturer.

Supplier

An external company or organisation that provides products. This can be equipment,
consumables or spares. For medical-equipment suppliers, who have the direct support of
the manufacturer for advice, spare parts and formal training of service technicians, and are
commonly referred to as official distributors.

Contractor

An external company or organisation which provides support services. This includes
installation, servicing, repair or (quality control) recertification.

Advisor

A subject-matter expert providing technical and strategic support to the operations on a
specific topic. Usually these positions are attached to an OC HQ and, together with similar
experts in other OCs, form the Working Groups to support strategy and collaboration on
their topic at movement-wide level and guidance to other speciality working groups.
Sometimes the French term “Referent” is also used.

Policy

A policy is a number of written statements that describe principles, requirements and
limitations. The statements are characterised by indicating "what" must be done rather than
"how" to do it. Such statements have the force of establishing rights, requirements and
responsibilities. A procedure is a set of mandatory steps providing specific how-to
information, aiming at an objective.

Protocol

A protocol is a plan for a course of action, often with step-by-step instructions and
illustrations for ease of comprehension.

Guidelines

Guidelines detail recommended or customary methods of working to accomplish an
objective. A guideline is not mandatory but is generally followed.
