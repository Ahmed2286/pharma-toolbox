SELECTION OF EQUIPMENT

Medical equipment will usually be requested by the clinical staff to improve the quality of
care or to expand into new activities. Choosing the right equipment to meet this need is a
collaborative effort.

Selecting new equipment requires specialised knowledge, so all proposals must be checked
to see if they meet the medical usage and technical support requirements. The Medical
Coordinator should discuss the choice with the Medical supervisor in the cell, who can
ensure inclusion of the Medical and Biomedical Advisors to help ensure optimal equipment
choices.

The equipment choice must meet the needs of the medical activities, be appropriate for the
situation in which it will be used and be as easy as possible to ensure continued use. This
means that the infrastructure and the skills to use and maintain it should already be present
or must be possible to achieve with additional investment. The Logistics Coordinator can
seek the advice of the Logistics supervisor in the cell and the technical referent for
Biomedical Support.

Adding new equipment to a project adds to the complexity of managing it. Staff must have
sufficient skills to use the equipment well, and the technical support requirements must also
be considered. Equipment models do change regularly; this can also result in changes of
consumables and accessories as these are not always compatible. Having multiple models
further complicates inventory follow-up, equipment use and support.

MSF catalogue items should be your first choice if they meet the requirements, and they
should be ordered through MSF European Supply Centres (ESCs). This gives a number of
advantages:

•
The equipment has already been medically and technically approved and field tested.
Related articles and requirements are stated in the catalogue.
•
Experienced (inter)national clinical staff are probably familiar with it.
•
The technical support is available through the Technical Referent.
•
Better prices and technical support can be negotiated.
•
Any required certification or service support contracts will give better guarantees.
•
Better follow up in case the equipment becomes subject to a Field Safety Notice
issued by the manufacturer.
•
Greater (intersectional) flexibility for training work exchanges.
•
Greater (intersectional) flexibility for equipment backup or donations in case of
closures or handovers.

Local Purchase can be an option, but only where MSF catalogue items do not meet the
requirements, or where local factors make supply through the international supply centre
unadvisable. Any purchase of medical equipment (including accessories, consumables and
spare parts) must follow the MSF International Policy on Procurement of Medical Products.

Local factors could include:

•
Importation difficulties (impossible or exaggeratedly expensive).
•
The ESC supplier will not provide service in the destination country (for equipment
which requires annual quality control recertification).
•
The equipment is so critical to the medical activities that fast, local support is
essential.
•
There is a plan to handover medical activities to a Ministry of Health or another
partner who must have a continued (local) source of consumables and spares. Of
course availability is only one aspect; the partner must have the funding and HR to
continue to use and maintain it too!

The first choice of equipment will often be the MSF standard items. In addition to retaining
many of the advantages listed previously you have the security that even if the local supplier
cannot deliver the spares and consumables, they can still be obtained through the MSF
Supply Centres. In some cases, it can be useful to follow local standards for equipment
choice as staff will be more familiar with it and in-country support will likely be available.

All suppliers of medical equipment, spare parts or consumables must be

validated by the Pharma Team at HQ.

Remember that manufacturers of medical equipment are commercial companies, so
sometimes equipment is offered cheap but requires expensive consumables, accessories or
servicing contracts. Check also if MSF Supply Centre consumables and parts can be used with
locally purchased equipment. Especially laboratory equipment sometime uses special
cartridges with integrated electronics which will not work on certain machines (e.g. different
versions for different countries/continents).
