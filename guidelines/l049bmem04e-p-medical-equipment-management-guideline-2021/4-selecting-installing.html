4 SELECTING & INSTALLING

This chapter covers the following topics:

•
Assessing existing equipment.
•
Selecting equipment (international/local).
•
Selection of local suppliers/service contractors.
•
Ensuring that facilities are suitable.
•
Power supply requirements.
•
Financial considerations.

There is no point having equipment which no one can use, is not suited to the conditions or
for which no spares or consumables are available. A careful analysis before ordering can help
avoid this happening.

Modifying or making medical equipment ourselves is strongly discouraged.

Not only are there safety risks for patients and staff, but there is no

guarantee that the functionality will be adequate.

ASSESSING EXISTING EQUIPMENT

In many cases where MSF runs a project in collaboration with another organisation, such as
a Ministry of Health, there will already be equipment available at a health facility. Usually,
this equipment is owned by the partner organisation, and this can influence MSF's ability to
ensure good care and maintenance. This can often have the advantage that staff may be
familiar with the equipment, and local support and supply of the required consumables may
also be available.

Before any further evaluation the clinical team must confirm that the equipment is suitable
for use in support of MSF’s activities in accordance with MSF protocols. Ideally it should be
equivalent to an item already on the Medical Standard List. If this is the case then a further
evaluation can be done, led by the medical and/or nursing supervisor with the support of
the pharmacist, the Biomedical Technician and potentially supply if local suppliers need to
be assessed.

Carefully assess the feasibility of using this equipment by asking these questions:

•
Is the equipment in good condition, i.e. not visibly damaged or missing parts?
•
Is the clinical staff familiar with it and able to use it well?
•
Is the user and technical documentation available?
•
Does the partner organisation have skilled staff performing regular maintenance?
•
Is there a service provider for technical support (servicing and repair) in country?
•
Are there up-to-date maintenance records?
•
Is there a stock of spares and consumables, and is there a reliable (local) supplier for
original parts and consumables?
