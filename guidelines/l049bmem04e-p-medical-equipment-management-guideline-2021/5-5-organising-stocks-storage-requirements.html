the equipment, such as those done by the lab staff to verify the calibration of haemoglobin
and glucose meters using control solutions.

For each item of equipment these files should be kept in the medical service and be

•
Cleaning follow-up sheets.

Training attendance sheets for staff training on specific equipment.

ORGANISING STOCKS & STORAGE REQUIREMENTS

The stocks you will have to consider for medical equipment fall into four main categories,
and their storage should be arranged to ensure the best accessibility to those who need it, in
the right quantities and at the right time. The categories are:

Accessories are the various parts that are needed to use a device and are selected
depending on their particular use, or are not essential but can be used as an option. An
example is the pulse oximeter which has various types of sensors which are selected for
patient type (adult, paediatric or neonatal), and various different colours of rubber
protection covers which may be used to more easily identify the medical service to which
the device belongs. Generally, accessories are reusable.

Consumables are the various parts which need changing between each patient or
periodically. Examples: consumables used by the clinicians are silicon tubing for connecting
patients to oxygen concentrators, adhesive tape for some types of pulse oximeter sensors,
mouthpieces for anaesthesia ventilators and various antibacterial filters. Other consumables
are used by the Biomedical Technician during routine maintenance work. These include the
antibacterial filter for the oxygen concentrator which is inaccessible without removing the
device’s cover.

Spare parts are replacement parts which are needed by the Biomedical Technician to service
or repair equipment either during routine maintenance or as a result of equipment failure.
This includes everything from valves, gaskets and seals to electronic and power-supply
components.

Back up equipment are all items of equipment that are kept in stock to replace damaged or
failed equipment in the health facility.

Storage condition requirements for all items are defined by the manufacturer and are
usually very similar as they are often made from (or contain parts which are made from) a
variety of plastics and rubber, contain precision mechanical parts, or they have electronic
components. The conditions in any storage area must prevent the components from
degrading before they can be used. Degradation is caused by heat, humidity, dust and
physical impact. Warehouse fires are generally also rather destructive, and extra defences
may be needed for the warehouse in case of zombie apocalypse.

The manufacturer’s documentation will give specific requirements for each device, but as a
general rule the suggested storage conditions for the majority of items are:

•
Temperature <50°C.
•
Humidity <85% non-condensing.
•
Dust-free.

If you move equipment from an air-conditioned storage area to a hot or
humid working environment, you should leave it for 10-30 minute before
turning it on to avoid condensation in the electronics. This could damage

the equipment.

Stock location, responsibility for monitoring and ordering and availability for those who need
it are all closely linked. Also see chapter §2: Roles & Responsibilities. The best division of
your stocks will depend partially on the size of the project and the arrangement of your
other stocks, but in most cases the following separation should be used:

Health Facility (Medical Services):

A small supply of medical consumables and accessories, enough to cover the period
between requests to the project’s central pharmacy. Critical backup equipment (contingency
plan in case of breakdown) may also be stored in the health facility, either in a medical
service or in a central location such as a dispensary depending on the hours of service and
the need to have equipment available quickly.

Biomedical Workshop:

A small supply of technical consumables and spare parts, enough to cover routine
maintenance needs according to the maintenance schedule. A selection of replacement
equipment to exchange while maintenance work is being carried out.

Project Medical Stock (Pharmacy):

The main stock of all medical equipment-related items (including accessories, consumables
and spare parts). Also the bulk of the backup (contingency plan) equipment.
