Antiseptic

Surgical handscrubbing
Surgical skin preparation (i.e. patient preoperative showering, antiseptic cleansing of the surgical
site)
Antiseptic cleansing of wounds

4% chlorhexidine (CHX) (di)gluconate antimicrobial soap (scrub solution)

There are different protocols, for information:

Wet hands and forearms; apply 5 ml of solution on hands and forearms and rub for 1 or 2 mins
(i.e. 30 seconds or 1 min for each side); scrub the nails of each hand for 30 seconds; rinse.
Apply 5 ml of solution again on hands and forearms and rub for 2 mins; rinse thoroughly; dry with
a sterile towel.

As close as possible to the time of surgery:

Wet the whole body and wash head, face and genital area with plain soap (do not use CHX
soap), rinse.
Apply CHX soap (except to head, face and genital area) and rub until the foam is white. Start at
the neck and move down, finishing with the feet.
Leave in contact a few minutes and rinse; dry with a clean towel; put on clean clothes.

Swab the surgical site for 1 or 2 mins, using sterile gauze soaked with sterile water and solution;
rinse with sterile water; dry with sterile gauze.

Clean the wound; rinse thoroughly with sterile water or sterile 0.9% NaCl.

POVIDONE IODINE 10%, aqueous solution

Last updated: January 2026

Therapeutic action

Indications

Forms and strengths

Use

Antisepsis prior to injections or punctures

Antisepsis prior to surgery (surgical site preparation)

Antisepsis of wounds and burns

Contra-indications, adverse effects, precautions

Antiseptic

Antisepsis of intact or broken skin and mucous membranes

10% povidone iodine (PVI) aqueous solution

Apply 10% solution to the injection/puncture site and allow to dry before inserting the needle. The
skin should be cleaned beforehand if soiled.
Some national infection prevention and control guidelines recommend to clean the skin beforehand
with PVI scrub prior to procedures associated with higher risk of infections (e.g. epidural/spinal
anaesthesia).

Application technique and number of applications vary depending on surgical site, type of
procedure and infection risk. For information:

The surgical site should be cleaned beforehand with PVI scrub.
Apply a sufficient amount of 10% solution to cover the incision site and surrounding area and

allow to dry before incision. If more than one application is required, allow to dry completely
between each application.

Apply 10% solution to small or superficial wounds and burns.
For extensive or deep wounds and burns, and wound irrigation:

Use a diluted solution: 1 part of 10% PVI + 3 parts of sterile water or sterile 0.9% NaCl;
Rinse thoroughly with sterile water or sterile 0.9% NaCl.

Storage

– Below 25 °C
Once opened, use the solution within 30 days.

Do not use with other antiseptics (risk of incompatibility) or mercury compounds (risk of necrosis).
Given the possible interactions between different antiseptics, PVI aqueous solution can only be
used with other PVI-based products (e.g. PVI scrub solution).
Do not use in preterm neonates or neonates < 1500 g, and use with caution in full-term neonates

and patients with extensive wounds or thyroid dysfunction (increased risk of systemic iodine
absorption and thyroid dysfunction).
May cause: thyroid dysfunction in susceptible patients, skin dryness and irritation with frequent use;
rarely, hypersensitivity reactions.
In case of contact with eyes, rinse immediately with water (risk of eye damage).
Pregnancy and breast-feeding: use with caution and only if clearly needed (passage through the
placenta and breast milk, risk of fetal or infant thyroid dysfunction). Avoid repeated use and
application on large areas.
